NEP-Wide field (North Ecliptic Pole Wide Field)

Description
- A well-studied extragalactic deep field centered near the North Ecliptic Pole, extensively observed from X-ray to radio (AKARI, Spitzer, WISE, Herschel, SCUBA-2, GALEX, DESI, Subaru HSC, etc.).
- Area: ~5.4 deg^2 for AKARI NEP-Wide; used as a target field for many infrared and optical surveys and planned missions like SPHEREx.

Relevance
- Dense multiwavelength coverage makes it ideal for studies of galaxy evolution, AGN demographics, and cross-survey calibration.
- Useful as a reference field for IR missions that will perform all-sky or wide-area spectroscopy.

Resources
- Photometric catalogs: AKARI IRC bands (2–25 μm), HSC/Subaru optical (g,r,i,z,y), WISE, Spitzer/IRAC, Herschel, SCUBA-2.
- Spectroscopic coverage: DESI DR1 (large numbers), Shim et al. (2013) Hectospec/Hydra observations.
