Concept: X-ray / optical offsets in merging clusters

Summary:
Offsets between the peak of X-ray emission (tracing hot, collisional intracluster gas) and the distribution of galaxies (collisionless) are a hallmark of cluster mergers observed near core passage. The displacement arises because ram pressure slows the gas while galaxies and dark matter pass relatively unimpeded. Measuring these offsets, combined with lensing, constrains the behavior of dark matter and the merger geometry.

Observational signatures:
- Spatial separation between X-ray centroid/peaks and galaxy luminosity peaks.
- Shock fronts and surface brightness discontinuities in X-rays.
- Asymmetric temperature maps and disturbed morphologies.

Applications:
- Constraints on dark matter self-interaction cross-section (e.g., Bullet cluster analyses).
- Timing of merger stage (pre-, near-, post-pericenter) and relative velocity estimates.

Example:
- 2603.10849 (Peanut cluster) reports X-ray substructure and offsets between X-ray peaks and galaxy density peaks, consistent with a near-pericenter major merger.
