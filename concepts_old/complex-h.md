Complex H (Southern Complex H, SCH)

Definition / identity:
- Complex H is a large high-velocity cloud complex in the second Galactic quadrant, containing very high-velocity gas (v_lsr ≲ -170 to -120 km/s). It extends over ~15 degrees on the sky and shows multi-phase HI structure.

New results from He et al. (arXiv:2603.10607):
- Embedded binary young clusters (Emei-1 and Emei-2) tied to a cold VHVC core (C1) at v_lsr ≈ -181 km/s.
- Stellar distance: 13.8 ± 0.6 kpc; links Complex H physically to the outer Galactic disk and implies a prograde, south-to-north orbit for SCH.
- Observed "slow-fast-slow" (SFS) latitude-velocity pattern across Complex H spanning ~100 km/s and ~15 degrees in latitude, interpreted as interaction/merging with disk gas and gas stripping processes.
- Cold cores in SCH (C1, C2, C3) have narrow velocity dispersions (~2 km/s) and cometary head-tail morphology aligned with cluster proper motions, consistent with motion-induced compression and cloud-cloud collision triggering star formation.

Physical parameters reported:
- C1 deconvolved size (channel 2): ~320 × 99 pc.
- Ambient disk gas density upper limit (from drag modeling): ~1e-24 g cm^-3.

Implications:
- Confirms that some HVCs can be sites of in-situ star formation; SCH provides a laboratory to study near-pristine, low-metallicity star formation and the process of gas accretion onto the Milky Way.
- Suggests that parts of Complex H are in the process of merging into the Galactic disk, with outer layers decelerating.

References:
- He et al., arXiv:2603.10607 (2026)
- EBHIS HI survey (Winkel et al. 2016) used for analysis
