Emei clusters (Emei-1 and Emei-2)

Identification:
- Two spatially compact, co-moving young open clusters discovered within the cold core C1 of Southern Complex H (SCH).

Measured properties (He et al., arXiv:2603.10607):
- Age: 11.2 ± 0.6 Myr (isochrone fitting; consistent with B-type main-sequence spectra for brightest members).
- Distance: 13.8 ± 0.6 kpc.
- Brightest member radial velocities: E-S1 v_lsr = -202 ± 5 km/s; E-S2 v_lsr = -158 ± 1 km/s; mean ~ -180 km/s.
- Stellar metallicities: Z_E-S1 ≈ 0.04^{+0.06}_{-0.02} Z_sun; Z_E-S2 ≈ 0.06^{+0.07}_{-0.03} Z_sun.
- Spatial separation between clusters: ~0.1 degrees on sky; projected separation ≲ ~20 pc from HI core peaks.

Interpretation and significance:
- Provide the first direct stellar distance and metallicity measurements for Complex H; secure association between stellar population and VHVC gas.
- Likely formed via an internal cloud-cloud collision in SCH; the clusters lie at the head of a cometary cold gas structure aligned with their proper motions.
- Rapid decoupling of stars from gas (<~20 Myr) explains the dearth of older stellar counterparts in HVC surveys.

Predicted fate:
- Orbit integration suggests clusters will enter high-inclination orbits and cross the Galactic disk within ~30 Myr; upcoming supernovae from massive members may reshape local ISM and possibly trigger further disk star formation.

Notes:
- Use Emei clusters as a template for searching other very young stellar groups associated with cold HI cores in HVCs.
