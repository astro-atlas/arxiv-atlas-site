Concept: Velocity-dispersion → Mass scaling for clusters

Summary:
Line-of-sight velocity dispersion (sigma_los) of cluster member galaxies is a commonly used dynamical proxy for total cluster mass. Empirical and simulation-calibrated scaling relations (e.g., Evrard et al. 2008) relate sigma to M200 or M500, but assumptions of virial equilibrium and dynamical relaxation may fail in mergers, biasing mass estimates.

Key points:
- For relaxed clusters, sigma_los scales with mass roughly as M200 ∝ sigma^3 (approximate virial expectation), but normalization and scatter depend on sample selection and dynamical state.
- In merging or non-virialized systems, velocity distributions can be multimodal or show large line-of-sight components, producing overestimated dispersions and inflated mass estimates.
- Complementary mass measures (X-ray hydrostatic, SZ, lensing) help triangulate true mass and identify biases.

Usage notes:
- Use with caution in systems showing clear substructure or disturbed X-ray morphology.
- Statistical tests for multimodality (KMM, DS-test) and removal of interlopers improve robustness.

Representative references:
- Evrard et al. (2008) — calibration of velocity dispersion–mass relation from simulations.
- 2603.10849 — example where sigma_los=1455±83 km/s implies M200~2e15 Msun, but merger state may bias this estimate.
