Single-epoch virial BH mass estimation (using BELs)

Concept
- Estimate MBH from single-epoch spectroscopy using the virial relation: MBH ∝ R_BLR × (ΔV)^2, where ΔV is the BEL velocity width (FWHM or sigma) and R_BLR is the broad-line region size inferred from a luminosity–size relation.

Practical steps
- Measure FWHM of broad emission lines (C IV, Mg II, Hβ, Hα) from spectra.
- Adopt a calibration relating continuum (or MIR) luminosity to R_BLR (from reverberation mapping samples).
- Combine to compute MBH; apply bolometric corrections to get Lbol and Eddington ratio.

Limitations
- Orientation, radiation pressure, and non-virial motions (especially for C IV) introduce scatter and systematic biases.
- Luminosity indicators subject to extinction (optical) or host contamination (NIR/MIR) unless corrected.

References
- Peterson (2004); McLure & Dunlop (2004); Kim et al. (2023) for MIR-based variants.
