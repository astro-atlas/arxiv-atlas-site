<p>
  The <strong>Sunyaev-Zeldovich (SZ) effect</strong> is the spectral distortion of cosmic microwave background (CMB) photons caused by inverse Compton scattering off hot electrons in the intracluster medium <a href="#ref1">[1]</a>. The thermal SZ effect creates a characteristic decrement at frequencies below ~217 GHz and increment above, providing a redshift-independent measure of the integrated electron pressure along the line of sight <a href="#ref1">[1]</a>.
</p>
<h2 id="mass-estimation">Use in Mass Estimation</h2>
<p>
  Combined with X-ray observations, the SZ effect constrains cluster masses without assuming isothermal gas <a href="#ref2">[2]</a>. For the <a href="/pages/coma-cluster" class="wikilink">Coma Cluster</a>, Mirakhor &amp; Walker (2020) combined XMM-Newton X-ray and Planck SZ data to obtain M<sub>200</sub> = (8.5 ± 0.55) × 10<sup>14</sup> M<sub>☉</sub> <a href="#ref2">[2]</a>.
</p>
