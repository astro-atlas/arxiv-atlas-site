<p>
  The <strong>Dark Energy Spectroscopic Instrument (DESI)</strong> is a massively multiplexed spectroscopic survey designed to measure baryon acoustic oscillations and the growth of structure <a href="#ref1">[1]</a>. Its Data Release 1 (DR1) provides millions of galaxy and quasar spectra <a href="#ref1">[1]</a>.
</p>
<h2 id="coma-application">Application to Coma Cluster</h2>
<p>
  DESI DR1 provides &gt;2000 spectra in a single pointing toward the <a href="/pages/coma-cluster" class="wikilink">Coma Cluster</a>, achieving 91% spectroscopic completeness <a href="#ref1">[1]</a><a href="#ref2">[2]</a>. This unprecedented sample enabled the kinematic analysis of Pedratti et al. (2026), which identified 1651 cluster members and measured population-dependent <a href="/pages/orbital-anisotropy-in-galaxy-clusters" class="wikilink">orbital anisotropy</a> <a href="#ref2">[2]</a>.
</p>
