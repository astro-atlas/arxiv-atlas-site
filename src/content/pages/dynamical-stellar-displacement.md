<p>
  <strong>Dynamical stellar displacement</strong> refers to the physical relocation of pre-existing stellar populations from their birthplace in a galaxy to large galactocentric distances through gravitational processes during <a href="/pages/galaxy-mergers" class="wikilink">galaxy mergers</a> and collisions<sup><a href="#ref1">[1]</a><a href="#ref2">[2]</a></sup>. Unlike <a href="/pages/ram-pressure-stripping" class="wikilink">ram-pressure stripping</a> (which primarily affects gas), dynamical displacement acts on both stars and gas through gravitational tidal forces and collisionally driven pressure waves<sup><a href="#ref1">[1]</a></sup>.
</p>
<h2>Mechanisms</h2>
<p>
  Two primary mechanisms drive dynamical stellar displacement:
</p>
<ul>
  <li><strong>Tidal stripping:</strong> During close passages or mergers, gravitational tidal forces strip stars from the outer regions of galaxies into tidal tails, bridges, and stellar streams<sup><a href="#ref3">[3]</a><a href="#ref4">[4]</a></sup>.</li>
  <li><strong>Collisional density waves:</strong> In <a href="/pages/collisional-ring-galaxies" class="wikilink">collisional ring galaxies</a>, the pressure wave generated by a head-on encounter sweeps both gas and pre-existing disk stars outward with the expanding ring<sup><a href="#ref1">[1]</a><a href="#ref5">[5]</a></sup>. This mechanism is distinct from tidal stripping in that it operates through a coherent, radially propagating density perturbation rather than differential gravitational pull.</li>
</ul>
<h2>Evidence in SN 2025adpq</h2>
<p>
  The discovery of SN 2025adpq provides compelling evidence for dynamical displacement of <a href="/pages/type-ia-supernovae" class="wikilink">Type Ia SN</a> progenitors<sup><a href="#ref2">[2]</a></sup>. The supernova lies at 11.4 kpc from G1 within the expanding collisional ring "Pika's Halo." Deep Ca H&amp;K absorption in the ring confirms the presence of old stars displaced from G1, while the host galaxy G1 formed the bulk of its stars ~11 Gyr ago<sup><a href="#ref2">[2]</a><a href="#ref6">[6]</a></sup>. Since the ring is only ~70&ndash;200 Myr old, far shorter than the &ge;0.5 Gyr <a href="/pages/supernova-delay-times" class="wikilink">delay time</a> of SN Ia progenitors<sup><a href="#ref7">[7]</a><a href="#ref8">[8]</a></sup>, the progenitor must have predated the ring and was swept outward during the collision<sup><a href="#ref2">[2]</a></sup>.
</p>
<h2>Broader Context</h2>
<p>
  Dynamical displacement is also relevant for producing the intracluster stellar population in galaxy clusters, where repeated tidal interactions strip stars from individual galaxies into the diffuse cluster potential<sup><a href="#ref9">[9]</a></sup>. The mechanism proposed by Ditrani et al. (2024)<sup><a href="#ref10">[10]</a></sup> generalizes this: galaxy interactions of all types can redistribute long-lived SN Ia progenitor systems to locations far from their formation sites.
</p>
<h2>Why This Matters</h2>
<p>
  Dynamical displacement fundamentally decouples the explosion site of a <a href="/pages/type-ia-supernovae" class="wikilink">Type Ia SN</a> from the environment where its progenitor formed. This means that the local stellar population at the SN site may not accurately reflect the progenitor's formation conditions &mdash; a critical consideration for studies that use SN environments to constrain progenitor channels<sup><a href="#ref2">[2]</a></sup>. It also provides a natural explanation for <a href="/pages/large-offset-supernovae" class="wikilink">large-offset</a> and <a href="/pages/hostless-supernovae" class="wikilink">hostless</a> SNe Ia without requiring exotic progenitor scenarios<sup><a href="#ref2">[2]</a></sup>.
</p>
