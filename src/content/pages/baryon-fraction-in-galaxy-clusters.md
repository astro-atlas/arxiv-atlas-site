<p>
  The <strong>baryon fraction</strong> f<sub>b</sub> in galaxy clusters — the ratio of baryonic (gas + stellar) mass to total mass — is expected to approximate the cosmic value Ω<sub>b</sub>/Ω<sub>m</sub> because clusters are large enough to retain a representative sample of the universe's matter content <a href="#ref1">[1]</a>.
</p>
<h2 id="coma">Coma Cluster Measurement</h2>
<p>
  Pedratti et al. (2026) derive f<sub>b</sub> = 0.15 ± 0.02 at r = 2.12 Mpc for the <a href="/pages/coma-cluster" class="wikilink">Coma Cluster</a>, consistent with the cosmological value <a href="#ref2">[2]</a><a href="#ref1">[1]</a>. The gas mass profile is estimated from XMM-Newton electron density measurements <a href="#ref3">[3]</a>.
</p>
