<p>
  <strong>Emission-line diagnostics</strong> use the ratios of nebular emission lines to classify the ionization source in galaxies and derive physical properties of the ionized gas. The most widely used tool is the BPT diagram (Baldwin, Phillips &amp; Terlevich 1981<sup><a href="#ref1">[1]</a></sup>), which plots [O III]/H&#946; against [N II]/H&#945; to separate star-forming regions, AGN, and composite/LINER ionization<sup><a href="#ref1">[1]</a><a href="#ref2">[2]</a></sup>.
</p>
<h2 id="bpt">BPT Diagram</h2>
<p>
  The BPT diagram separates ionization mechanisms because the harder radiation field of AGN produces stronger [O III] and [N II] relative to Balmer lines compared to HII regions ionized by young stars<sup><a href="#ref1">[1]</a></sup>. Additional diagnostic diagrams use [S II]/H&#945; and [O I]/H&#945; ratios. In retired galaxies (e.g., M51b), weak emission powered by hot low-mass evolved stars (HOLMES) falls in the LINER/retired region of the BPT diagram<sup><a href="#ref2">[2]</a><a href="#ref3">[3]</a></sup>.
</p>
<h2 id="metallicity">Gas-Phase Metallicity from Emission Lines</h2>
<p>
  Strong-line calibrations use emission-line ratios to estimate nebular (gas-phase) oxygen abundance, 12 + log(O/H). Common indicators include O3N2 = log([O III]/H&#946; / [N II]/H&#945;) and the N2 index<sup><a href="#ref4">[4]</a></sup>. These are used to construct <a href="/pages/metallicity-gradients" class="wikilink">metallicity gradient</a> maps in spatially resolved studies<sup><a href="#ref5">[5]</a><a href="#ref6">[6]</a></sup>.
</p>
<h2 id="halpha">H&#945; as Star Formation Tracer</h2>
<p>
  H&#945; emission traces ionizing photons from massive O/B stars, making the H&#945; equivalent width (EW) a direct indicator of specific <a href="/pages/star-formation" class="wikilink">star formation</a> rate. In the M51 group, spatially resolved H&#945; EW maps reveal star-forming spiral arms in M51a and retired regions in M51b, while M63 shows asymmetric H&#945; patterns consistent with outside-in <a href="/pages/quenching-star-formation" class="wikilink">quenching</a><sup><a href="#ref6">[6]</a></sup>.
</p>
