<p>
  <strong>Projected phase space (PPS)</strong> is the two-dimensional space of projected clustercentric radius R and line-of-sight velocity v<sub>los</sub> for cluster member galaxies <a href="#ref1">[1]</a>. It is the fundamental observable for kinematic analyses of galaxy clusters, as 3D positions and velocities cannot be directly measured <a href="#ref1">[1]</a>.
</p>
<h2 id="applications">Applications</h2>
<p>
  PPS data is the input for <a href="/pages/mamposst" class="wikilink">MAMPOSSt</a> and similar codes that solve the <a href="/pages/jeans-equation" class="wikilink">Jeans equation</a> to jointly constrain mass and <a href="/pages/orbital-anisotropy-in-galaxy-clusters" class="wikilink">anisotropy</a> profiles <a href="#ref1">[1]</a>. The location of galaxies in PPS correlates with their infall history: recently accreted galaxies occupy the high-velocity, large-radius regions, while virialised members cluster near the centre with moderate velocities <a href="#ref1">[1]</a><a href="#ref2">[2]</a>.
</p>
<h2 id="interloper-removal">Interloper Removal</h2>
<p>
  Foreground and background galaxies contaminate the PPS. The Clean algorithm of Mamon et al. (2013) provides refined interloper removal superior to simple phase-space cuts <a href="#ref1">[1]</a>.
</p>
