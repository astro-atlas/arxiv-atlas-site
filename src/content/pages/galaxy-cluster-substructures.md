<p>
  <strong>Galaxy cluster substructures</strong> are dynamically distinct groups, filaments, or infalling systems within or around a cluster <a href="#ref1">[1]</a>. Their presence indicates that a cluster is not fully relaxed, with implications for mass estimates and galaxy evolution <a href="#ref1">[1]</a><a href="#ref2">[2]</a>.
</p>
<h2 id="coma">Substructure in the Coma Cluster</h2>
<p>
  The <a href="/pages/coma-cluster" class="wikilink">Coma Cluster</a> was long considered a prototype relaxed system <a href="#ref1">[1]</a>, but extensive substructure is now recognized:
</p>
<ul>
  <li><strong>NGC 4839 group:</strong> Identified in X-ray maps <a href="#ref3">[3]</a><a href="#ref4">[4]</a> and galaxy distributions <a href="#ref5">[5]</a>, on secondary infall after first apocentre <a href="#ref6">[6]</a><a href="#ref7">[7]</a><a href="#ref8">[8]</a></li>
  <li><strong>NGC 4889/4874 merger:</strong> Recent or ongoing major merger between the two BCG parent groups <a href="#ref9">[9]</a></li>
  <li><strong>Faint subgroups:</strong> Many additional faint substructures recently identified <a href="#ref2">[2]</a><a href="#ref10">[10]</a></li>
</ul>
<h2 id="impact">Impact on Analysis</h2>
<p>
  Substructure contaminates kinematic mass estimates by violating the equilibrium assumption <a href="#ref11">[11]</a>. Velocity distributions of unrelaxed systems depart significantly from Gaussianity <a href="#ref12">[12]</a><a href="#ref13">[13]</a><a href="#ref14">[14]</a>. The Anderson-Darling test quantifies these departures <a href="#ref15">[15]</a>. Post-starburst galaxies in the Coma periphery may be driven by substructure-related interactions <a href="#ref16">[16]</a><a href="#ref17">[17]</a>.
</p>
