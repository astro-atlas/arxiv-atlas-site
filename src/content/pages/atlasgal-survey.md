<p>
  <strong>ATLASGAL</strong> (APEX Telescope Large Area Survey of the Galaxy) is an unbiased 870 μm continuum survey of the inner Galactic plane conducted with the LABOCA bolometer array on the 12-m APEX telescope in Chile<sup><a href="#ref1">[1]</a></sup>. It provides the most comprehensive census of dense <a href="/pages/dust" class="wikilink">dust</a> clumps in the Milky Way, including the progenitors of <a href="/pages/star-formation" class="wikilink">star formation</a> across all evolutionary stages.
</p>
<h2 id="section-0">Survey Products</h2>
<p>
  The ATLASGAL compact source catalog contains ~8000 dense clumps with measured velocities, kinematic distances, luminosities, and masses<sup><a href="#ref2">[2]</a></sup>. At 870 μm, the <a href="/pages/dust" class="wikilink">dust</a> emission is optically thin, making it an excellent tracer of total gas <a href="/pages/column-density" class="wikilink">column density</a> and mass.
</p>
<h2 id="section-1">Role in Star Formation Studies</h2>
<p>
  ATLASGAL clumps serve as the parent sample for targeted follow-up studies of <a href="/pages/magnetic-fields-in-star-formation" class="wikilink">magnetic fields</a>, kinematics, and fragmentation in high-mass star-forming regions. For G351.77-0.53, ATLASGAL identified the embedded clumps (c1–c6) along the <a href="/pages/filamentary-star-formation" class="wikilink">filament</a>, with clump c1 hosting the massive protocluster IRAS 17233-3606<sup><a href="#ref3">[3]</a></sup>.
</p>
