<div class="key-takeaways">
  <div class="key-takeaways-title">Key Takeaways</div>
  <h4>Main Results</h4>
  <ul>
    <li>All RM values in 3C 84 from 1–15 pc follow a single power law RM ∝ r<sup>−2.7±0.2</sup> (Pearson r = −0.876, p = 2×10<sup>−9</sup>), unifying two decades of apparently discrepant measurements from KVN, VLBA, and HSA <a href="#ref1">[1]</a>.</li>
    <li>The Faraday screen is the circumnuclear ambient medium filling the Bondi sphere (r &lt; 11.8 pc), ruling out a RIAF <a href="#ref15">[15]</a>, jet boundary layer <a href="#ref16">[16]</a>, and localized gas cloud <a href="#ref17">[17]</a> via spatial extent, sign stability, and cold-gas coexistence arguments <a href="#ref1">[1]</a>.</li>
    <li>First spatially resolved ambient B-field measurement in an elliptical galaxy: B ∝ r<sup>−1.7 to −2.2</sup> within the Bondi radius, from ~0.1–1.5 μG at 12 pc to mG–G scales near the core, given n<sub>e</sub> ∝ r<sup>−1.5 to −2</sup> <a href="#ref1">[1]</a>.</li>
    <li>The RM power law is much steeper than M87 (k ~ 1 <a href="#ref6">[6]</a>), suggesting fundamentally different circumnuclear environments between these two nearby radio galaxies <a href="#ref1">[1]</a>.</li>
  </ul>
  <h4>Implications</h4>
  <p>This result challenges the default assumption that large RMs in low-luminosity AGN trace RIAFs or accretion disks — the ambient circumnuclear medium can dominate Faraday rotation. The spatially resolved B-field profile provides a direct observational anchor for MHD models of AGN fueling and feedback in cool-core clusters like Perseus. For galaxy evolution, understanding the magnetic environment at parsec scales constrains how AGN jets interact with and heat the surrounding medium, a critical ingredient in the feedback cycle that regulates <a href="/pages/star-formation" class="wikilink">star formation</a> in massive ellipticals.</p>
  <h4>Other Works</h4>
  <p>Earlier RM studies of 3C 84 by Plambeck et al. (2014) with CARMA/SMA and Nagai et al. (2017) with VLBA measured individual components but could not identify a unified trend. The RIAF interpretation favored by Plambeck et al. and the jet boundary layer model of Kim et al. (2019) are both superseded by this ambient-medium picture. The steep RM ∝ r<sup>−2.7</sup> contrasts with M87's shallow profile (Zavala &amp; Taylor 2002), pointing to a diversity in circumnuclear magnetization among low-luminosity AGN. Trippe et al. (2012) first suggested the ambient medium as Faraday screen origin — this work provides the definitive confirmation with spatially resolved data.</p>
</div>
<h2 id="summary">Summary</h2>
<p>
  This paper presents multi-frequency polarimetric VLBI observations of 3C 84 (the radio source in NGC 1275) spanning 8–141 GHz from 2015–2024, using the Korean VLBI Network (KVN), Very Long Baseline Array (VLBA), and High Sensitivity Array (HSA) <a href="#ref1">[1]</a>. The authors detect linear polarization from multiple inner jet components spanning 1–15 pc from the black hole and show that all <a href="/pages/faraday-rotation" class="wikilink">rotation measure (RM)</a> values follow a single power-law RM &prop; r<sup>&minus;2.7&plusmn;0.2</sup> <a href="#ref1">[1]</a>. This unified relation is naturally explained by a common circumnuclear ambient medium within the <a href="/pages/bondi-accretion" class="wikilink">Bondi radius</a> (~11.8 pc) of NGC 1275, providing the first spatially resolved measurement of the ambient <a href="/pages/magnetic-field" class="wikilink">magnetic field</a> in an elliptical galaxy <a href="#ref1">[1]</a>.
</p>
<h2 id="key-findings">Key Findings</h2>
<h3>Unified RM&ndash;Distance Power Law</h3>
<p>
  All RM measurements of <a href="/pages/3c84-ngc1275-jet" class="wikilink">3C 84</a> from 1–15 pc — spanning inner jet components C4a, C4, C3, K, and the southern lobe — follow a single power-law RM &prop; r<sup>&minus;2.7&plusmn;0.2</sup> with Pearson r = &minus;0.876, p = 2.1&times;10<sup>&minus;9</sup> <a href="#ref1">[1]</a>. This unifies RM measurements that had previously seemed discrepant across multiple instruments and two decades of observations <a href="#ref2">[2]</a><a href="#ref3">[3]</a><a href="#ref4">[4]</a><a href="#ref5">[5]</a>. The power-law is much steeper than the RM profile of M87 (k~1) <a href="#ref6">[6]</a>.
</p>
<h3>Circumnuclear Ambient Medium as Faraday Screen</h3>
<p>
  The paper establishes that the <a href="/pages/faraday-rotation" class="wikilink">Faraday screen</a> is the circumnuclear ambient medium filling the Bondi sphere (r &lt; 11.8 pc) <a href="#ref1">[1]</a>. Three lines of evidence disfavor competing hypotheses:
</p>
<ul>
  <li><strong><a href="/pages/riaf-adaf-models" class="wikilink">RIAF</a> disfavored:</strong> The coexistence of BLR emission lines and FFA disk absorption requires cold gas (~10<sup>4</sup> K) that would be destroyed if a RIAF extended to parsec scales <a href="#ref1">[1]</a><a href="#ref7">[7]</a><a href="#ref8">[8]</a>. The Eddington ratio (~0.04–0.09%) places 3C 84 near the accretion state transition <a href="#ref1">[1]</a><a href="#ref9">[9]</a>.</li>
  <li><strong>Jet boundary layer disfavored:</strong> Consistently positive RM sign (no temporal sign reversals) contrasts with behavior expected for dynamical jet sheaths <a href="#ref1">[1]</a><a href="#ref5">[5]</a>. RM values &gt; 10<sup>5</sup> rad/m<sup>2</sup> are unusual for boundary layers in nearby AGN <a href="#ref1">[1]</a>.</li>
  <li><strong>Gas cloud disfavored:</strong> Persistent RM after hotspot disruption and spatially extended RM across multiple components rule out a localized origin <a href="#ref1">[1]</a><a href="#ref4">[4]</a><a href="#ref10">[10]</a>.</li>
</ul>
<h3>First Spatially Resolved Ambient B-Field in an Elliptical Galaxy</h3>
<p>
  By combining the RM profile with electron density models (n<sub>e</sub> &prop; r<sup>&minus;1.5 to &minus;2</sup>), the paper constrains B &prop; r<sup>&minus;1.7 to &minus;2.2</sup> within the Bondi radius <a href="#ref1">[1]</a>. At the Bondi radius (11.8 pc), B = 0.1–1.5 &mu;G, increasing rapidly inward toward mG–G near the VLBI core <a href="#ref1">[1]</a>. Previous elliptical galaxy magnetic field measurements were galaxy-averaged or theoretical <a href="#ref11">[11]</a><a href="#ref12">[12]</a><a href="#ref13">[13]</a>.
</p>
<h3>New Polarization Detections</h3>
<p>
  First polarization detections from jet components C4 (emerging robustly in 2022 December at RM = 2.6–3.5&times;10<sup>5</sup> rad/m<sup>2</sup>) and K (RM = 0.5–0.9&times;10<sup>5</sup> rad/m<sup>2</sup>) <a href="#ref1">[1]</a>. The consistent ordering C4 &gt; C3 &gt; K within the same epochs establishes a clear distance-dependent RM gradient <a href="#ref1">[1]</a>. HSA 8 GHz confirmed the southern lobe RM (~7000 rad/m<sup>2</sup>) is unchanged from the Taylor (2006) measurement 20 years prior <a href="#ref2">[2]</a>.
</p>
<h2 id="methodology">Methodology</h2>
<h3>Multi-Frequency Polarimetric VLBI</h3>
<p>
  The KVN's simultaneous multi-frequency capability (43–141 GHz) is uniquely suited for large RM measurements because narrow frequency spacing resolves the n&pi; ambiguity <a href="#ref1">[1]</a><a href="#ref14">[14]</a>. An upgraded 16-Gbps OCTAD recording system (post-2022) enabled simultaneous 4-band observations <a href="#ref1">[1]</a>. D-term calibration used the GPCAL pipeline <a href="#ref15">[15]</a><a href="#ref16">[16]</a><a href="#ref17">[17]</a>, yielding significantly better accuracy than standard AIPS LPCAL.
</p>
<p>
  The VLBA BU monitoring program provided 18 epochs at 43 GHz (2015–2017) <a href="#ref18">[18]</a><a href="#ref19">[19]</a>. HSA 8 GHz observations (2023 December) combined all ten VLBA stations, phased VLA, and Effelsberg for superb sensitivity at the southern lobe <a href="#ref1">[1]</a>. PolConvert handled linear-circular feed basis conversion for Effelsberg data <a href="#ref20">[20]</a>.
</p>
<h3>External Faraday Rotation Confirmation</h3>
<p>
  EVPA rotations exceeding 45° with good &lambda;<sup>2</sup> fit (e.g., &gt;150° rotation between 86 and 141 GHz) conclusively indicate an external Faraday screen <a href="#ref1">[1]</a><a href="#ref21">[21]</a>. Internal <a href="/pages/faraday-rotation" class="wikilink">Faraday rotation</a> would deviate from &lambda;<sup>2</sup> for rotations above ~45° <a href="#ref21">[21]</a><a href="#ref22">[22]</a>. Bayesian linear regression with LINMIX <a href="#ref23">[23]</a> was used for the power-law fitting.
</p>
<h2 id="context">Literature Context</h2>
<h3>Prior RM Measurements of 3C 84</h3>
<p>
  Trippe et al. (2010, 2012) found no polarization at 81–348 GHz with IRAM PdBI, placing upper limits requiring RM dispersion &ge; 10<sup>6</sup> rad/m<sup>2</sup>, and first proposed the circumnuclear ambient medium as the Faraday screen origin <a href="#ref3">[3]</a><a href="#ref24">[24]</a>. Plambeck et al. (2014) detected 1–3% polarization at 1.3 mm with CARMA/SMA and measured RM = 8.7&times;10<sup>5</sup> rad/m<sup>2</sup>, attributing it to a RIAF or flattened disk within 0.4 pc <a href="#ref4">[4]</a>. Nagai et al. (2017) found RM = (3–6)&times;10<sup>5</sup> rad/m<sup>2</sup> at C3 with VLBA 43 GHz <a href="#ref25">[25]</a>. Kim et al. (2019) proposed a jet boundary layer as a more natural Faraday screen <a href="#ref5">[5]</a>. Taylor (2006) measured RM = 7000 rad/m<sup>2</sup> at the southern lobe <a href="#ref2">[2]</a>.
</p>
<h3>Accretion Physics</h3>
<p>
  The paper engages extensively with <a href="/pages/riaf-adaf-models" class="wikilink">RIAF/ADAF theory</a>: the foundational ADAF framework <a href="#ref7">[7]</a><a href="#ref26">[26]</a>, hot accretion flow reviews <a href="#ref8">[8]</a>, and the accretion state transition threshold <a href="#ref9">[9]</a>. For M87 and Sgr A*, RIAFs are the accepted Faraday screen <a href="#ref6">[6]</a><a href="#ref27">[27]</a><a href="#ref28">[28]</a><a href="#ref29">[29]</a>, but 3C 84's Eddington ratio and cold gas structures challenge this assumption here <a href="#ref1">[1]</a>.
</p>
<h3>AGN Environment</h3>
<p>
  NGC 1275 sits at the center of the Perseus cluster cool core <a href="#ref30">[30]</a><a href="#ref31">[31]</a>. X-ray electron density profiles from Fabian et al. (2006) give n<sub>e</sub> &prop; r<sup>&minus;0.67</sup> on kpc scales <a href="#ref30">[30]</a>, providing the outer boundary for the Bondi-sphere model. Optical emission-line filaments extending &gt;100 kpc carry magnetic fields of ~24 &mu;G <a href="#ref32">[32]</a><a href="#ref33">[33]</a>. The BLR disappears below L<sub>Bol</sub>/L<sub>Edd</sub> ~ 0.1–1% <a href="#ref34">[34]</a>, consistent with 3C 84's borderline status.
</p>
<h2 id="supports">What This Paper Supports</h2>
<ul>
  <li><strong>Trippe (2012) suggestion</strong> that the circumnuclear ambient medium is the Faraday screen origin — now confirmed with spatially resolved VLBI data <a href="#ref3">[3]</a></li>
  <li><strong>Taylor (2006) southern lobe RM</strong> — confirmed 20 years later and shown to lie on the same power law, demonstrating long-term stability <a href="#ref2">[2]</a></li>
  <li><strong>Burn (1966) external rotation criterion</strong> — EVPA rotation &gt;45° with good &lambda;<sup>2</sup> fit confirmed observationally <a href="#ref21">[21]</a></li>
  <li><strong><a href="/pages/bondi-accretion" class="wikilink">Bondi accretion</a> theory</strong> — free-fall density profile n<sub>e</sub> &prop; r<sup>&minus;1.5</sup> within the Bondi radius is a consistent model <a href="#ref35">[35]</a><a href="#ref36">[36]</a></li>
</ul>
<h2 id="challenges">What This Paper Challenges</h2>
<ul>
  <li><strong>Plambeck (2014):</strong> The assumption that CARMA/SMA RM comes from within 0.4 pc (a RIAF or flattened disk) is challenged — the polarized emission was likely dominated by unresolved C3, not the core <a href="#ref1">[1]</a><a href="#ref4">[4]</a></li>
  <li><strong>Kim (2019):</strong> The jet boundary layer as dominant Faraday screen is disfavored by consistently positive RM sign and spatial coherence across all components <a href="#ref1">[1]</a><a href="#ref5">[5]</a></li>
  <li><strong>Paraschos (2024, EHT):</strong> Core RM estimate of 6&times;10<sup>6</sup> rad/m<sup>2</sup> may be unreliable because unresolved ALMA polarization is dominated by downstream jet components, not the core <a href="#ref1">[1]</a><a href="#ref37">[37]</a></li>
  <li><strong>Common Eddington ratio of 0.4%:</strong> Revised downward to ~0.04–0.09% through better SED and M<sub>BH</sub> assumptions <a href="#ref1">[1]</a><a href="#ref4">[4]</a></li>
</ul>
<h2 id="limitations">Limitations</h2>
<ul>
  <li>Single source study — generalizability to other low-luminosity AGN remains to be tested <a href="#ref1">[1]</a></li>
  <li>Magnetic field geometry (radial vs. toroidal vs. turbulent) only weakly constrained by RM profile alone <a href="#ref1">[1]</a></li>
  <li>Core polarization remains undetected — the innermost RM regime (&lt;1 pc) is inaccessible <a href="#ref1">[1]</a></li>
  <li>Bondi-sphere model assumes spherical symmetry and steady-state accretion <a href="#ref35">[35]</a></li>
</ul>
