# arXiv:2603.09670

## The Metallicity Gradient Evolution of Star-Forming Galaxies

**Authors:** J. M. G. G. Jones, A. M. Swinbank, I. Smail, C. M. Harrison, D. M. Alexander, A. C. Edge, R. J. Ivison, T. A. Targett, J. L. Wardlow

**Published:** 2026-03-11

**Categories:** astro-ph.GA

**DOI:** 10.48550/arXiv.2603.09670

---

## Abstract

We present spatially resolved metallicity measurements for 320 star-forming galaxies at z~0.5-2.5 from the KMOS Deep Survey. We find that metallicity gradients flatten with cosmic time, evolving from Δlog(O/H)/Δlog(r) ≈ -0.4 at z~2 to ≈ -0.2 at z~0.5. The gradient evolution is mass-dependent, with more massive galaxies showing steeper initial gradients and slower evolution. We interpret this as evidence for inside-out growth, with central regions forming first and outer regions accreting metal-poor gas at later times.

---

## Key Findings

- **320 galaxies** at z~0.5-2.5 with resolved metallicities
- **Gradient evolution**: flattens from -0.4 (z~2) to -0.2 (z~0.5)
- **Mass dependence**: massive galaxies have steeper initial gradients
- **Inside-out growth**: central regions form first
- **Late accretion**: outer regions gain metal-poor gas

---

## Methods

- KMOS Deep Survey integral field spectroscopy
- Hα and [NII] metallicity diagnostics
- Spatially resolved metallicity mapping
- Gradient fitting and evolution analysis
- Mass-dependent trend analysis

---

## Relevance to Galaxy Evolution

Metallicity gradients encode information about galaxy assembly history. The observed evolution supports inside-out growth models and provides constraints on gas accretion timescales.
