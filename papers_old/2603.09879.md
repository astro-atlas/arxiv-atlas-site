## The MUSE Deep Survey: Star Formation Histories of z~1 Galaxies

**Authors:** T. Contini, B. Epinat, M. Puech, N. Bouché, R. Bacon, P. M. Weilbacher, L. Wisotzki, C. Adami, S. Bellocchi, J. Brinchmann, et al.

**Published:** 2026-03-11

**Categories:** astro-ph.GA

**DOI:** 10.48550/arXiv.2603.09879

---

## Abstract

We present star formation histories (SFHs) for 412 galaxies at z~1 from the MUSE Deep Survey. Using full spectral fitting with Bayesian techniques, we reconstruct SFHs spanning 0-10 Gyr lookback time. We find that massive galaxies (M*>10^10.5 M☉) formed 70% of their stellar mass by z~2, while lower mass galaxies continued forming stars to later times. The SFH diversity increases with decreasing mass, with low-mass galaxies showing both bursty and continuous star formation modes.

---

## Key Findings

- **412 galaxies** at z~1 with reconstructed SFHs
- **Massive galaxies**: 70% of stellar mass formed by z~2
- **Low-mass galaxies**: continued star formation to later times
- **SFH diversity increases** with decreasing mass
- **Two modes**: bursty vs. continuous star formation in low-mass galaxies
- **Bayesian full spectral fitting** used for SFH reconstruction

---

## Methods

- MUSE Deep Survey integral field spectroscopy
- Full spectral fitting with Bayesian approach
- Star formation history reconstruction
- Mass-dependent SFH analysis
- Lookback time coverage: 0-10 Gyr

---

## Relevance to Galaxy Evolution

This work provides direct constraints on when galaxies formed their stars, showing clear mass-dependent downsizing. The diversity of SFHs in low-mass galaxies suggests varied formation pathways.
