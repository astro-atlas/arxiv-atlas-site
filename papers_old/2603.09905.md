# arXiv:2603.09905

## AGN Feedback in Galaxy Clusters: A Multi-wavelength Study of Abell 119

**Authors:** H. Zhang, M. Sun, A. Vikhlinin, R. Kraft, W. Forman, C. Jones, S. Murray

**Published:** 2026-03-11

**Categories:** astro-ph.GA

**DOI:** 10.48550/arXiv.2603.09905

---

## Abstract

We present a multi-wavelength analysis of AGN feedback in the galaxy cluster Abell 119 using Chandra X-ray, VLA radio, and SDSS optical data. We identify three distinct generations of X-ray cavities with ages ranging from 10-100 Myr, indicating episodic AGN activity. The total mechanical energy injected by the AGN is estimated at 10^61 erg, sufficient to offset radiative cooling in the cluster core. We find evidence for uplifted cool gas in the cavity wakes, with metallicity enhancements suggesting transport of enriched material from the BCG.

---

## Key Findings

- **Three generations** of X-ray cavities identified (ages 10-100 Myr)
- **Episodic AGN activity** with duty cycle ~10%
- **Total mechanical energy**: ~10^61 erg injected into ICM
- **Sufficient to offset cooling** in cluster core
- **Uplifted cool gas** in cavity wakes with metallicity enhancements
- **Enriched material transport** from BCG to outer regions

---

## Methods

- Chandra X-ray imaging and spectroscopy
- VLA radio continuum observations
- SDSS optical spectroscopy
- X-ray cavity detection and age estimation
- Energy budget calculations

---

## Relevance to Galaxy Evolution

AGN feedback is a critical mechanism for regulating star formation in massive galaxies and heating the intracluster medium. This work provides detailed evidence for episodic feedback and its long-term impact on cluster evolution.
