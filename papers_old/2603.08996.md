# arXiv:2603.08996

## The Evolution of Galaxy Sizes from z~3 to z~0: A Mass-Dependent Analysis

**Authors:** A. van der Wel, M. Franx, P. G. van Dokkum, R. E. Skelton, I. Labbé, S. Wuyts, G. Brammer, M. T. Sargent, P. Lang, P. Oesch, et al.

**Published:** 2026-03-11

**Categories:** astro-ph.GA

**DOI:** 10.48550/arXiv.2603.08996

---

## Abstract

We study the evolution of galaxy sizes from z~3 to z~0 using 10,000 galaxies from CANDELS and 3D-HST. We find that size evolution is mass-dependent: low-mass galaxies (M* ~ 10^9.5 M☉) grow by a factor of ~3, while massive galaxies (M* ~ 10^11.5 M☉) grow by only ~30%. The size growth rate declines with time, with most growth occurring at z>1. We interpret this as evidence for minor merger-driven growth, with low-mass galaxies experiencing more mergers relative to their mass.

---

## Key Findings

- **10,000 galaxies** from CANDELS and 3D-HST
- **Mass-dependent size evolution**:
  - Low-mass (M* ~ 10^9.5 M☉): factor of ~3 growth
  - Massive (M* ~ 10^11.5 M☉): ~30% growth
- **Growth rate declines** with time
- **Most growth at z>1**
- **Minor merger-driven**: low-mass galaxies have more mergers relative to mass

## Methods

- CANDELS and 3D-HST HST imaging
- Structural parameter fitting (GALFIT)
- Size measurements (effective radii)
- Mass-size relation evolution
- Merger rate estimates

---

## Relevance to Galaxy Evolution

Size evolution is a fundamental observable in galaxy formation. The mass-dependent nature suggests different growth mechanisms dominate at different masses, with mergers playing a larger role for low-mass galaxies.
