# arXiv:2603.08830

## The Stellar-to-Halo Mass Relation: Constraints from Weak Lensing

**Authors:** R. Mandelbaum, A. Tasitsiomi, U. Seljak, A. Kravtsov, R. H. Wechsler

**Published:** 2026-03-11

**Categories:** astro-ph.GA

**DOI:** 10.48550/arXiv.2603.08830

---

## Abstract

We present new constraints on the stellar-to-halo mass relation (SHMR) using weak gravitational lensing measurements from the DES and HSC surveys. We find a peak in the stellar mass fraction at M_halo ~ 10^12 M☉, with lower fractions at both higher and lower halo masses. The SHMR evolves weakly with redshift, with the peak shifting to higher masses at higher z. Our results are consistent with a model where feedback and gravitational potential jointly regulate star formation efficiency across cosmic time.

---

## Key Findings

- **Weak lensing constraints** from DES and HSC surveys
- **Peak stellar mass fraction**: at M_halo ~ 10^12 M☉
- **Lower fractions**: at M_halo < 10^11.5 and > 10^12.5 M☉
- **Weak redshift evolution**: peak shifts to higher masses at higher z
- **Feedback + gravity**: jointly regulate star formation efficiency

---

## Methods

- Weak gravitational lensing (galaxy-galaxy lensing)
- DES and HSC survey data
- Halo mass estimates from lensing
- Stellar mass estimates from SED fitting
- SHMR fitting and evolution analysis

---

## Relevance to Galaxy Evolution

The SHMR is a fundamental relation connecting galaxies to their dark matter halos. Understanding its shape and evolution provides constraints on feedback processes and star formation regulation.
