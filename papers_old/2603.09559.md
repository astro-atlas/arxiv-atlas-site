## The Connection Between Galaxy Spin and Morphology

**Authors:** L. Cortese, A. Boselli, S. Boissier, J. Donas, B. F. Madore, P. Marcum, Y. Wada, T. M. Heckman, A. Gil de Paz, K. Schawinski, et al.

**Published:** 2026-03-11

**Categories:** astro-ph.GA

## Abstract

We investigate the relationship between galaxy spin parameter (λ) and morphology using 5,000 galaxies from the GALEX-SDSS-WISE Legacy Catalog. We find a clear bimodality in spin-morphology space: early-type galaxies cluster at low spin (λ < 0.5) while late-types span a wider range with higher average spin (λ ≈ 0.6). The transition between morphological classes occurs at λ ≈ 0.5, suggesting spin is a fundamental parameter governing galaxy structure. We propose that spin determines the relative importance of ordered rotation versus random motions in galaxy dynamics.

## Key Findings

- **5,000 galaxies** from GALEX-SDSS-WISE catalog

- **Transition point**: λ ≈ 0.5 between morphological classes
- **Spin as fundamental parameter**: governs galaxy structure
- **Ordered vs. random motions**: spin determines dynamical state
