# arXiv:2603.09015

## The Impact of AGN Feedback on the Stellar Populations of Massive Galaxies

**Authors:** M. Schirmer, G. B. Dalton, I. Smail, A. M. Swinbank, R. J. Ivison, J. P. Stott, C. M. Harrison, D. M. Alexander, A. D. Bunker

**Published:** 2026-03-11

**Categories:** astro-ph.GA

**DOI:** 10.48550/arXiv.2603.09015

---

## Abstract

We investigate how AGN feedback affects stellar populations in 200 massive galaxies (M* > 10^11 M☉) at z~0.5-1. Using deep spectroscopy, we find that galaxies with luminous AGN (L_bol > 10^45 erg/s) show older stellar populations and higher [α/Fe] ratios than matched control galaxies without AGN. The age difference is ~1-2 Gyr, suggesting AGN activity truncates star formation earlier. The enhanced α-enhancement indicates rapid star formation prior to quenching, consistent with AGN feedback driving rapid quenching in massive galaxies.

---

## Key Findings

- **200 massive galaxies** (M* > 10^11 M☉) at z~0.5-1
- **AGN galaxies**: older stellar populations by ~1-2 Gyr
- **Higher [α/Fe] ratios**: in AGN hosts
- **Truncated star formation**: AGN activity quenches earlier
- **Rapid quenching**: enhanced α-enhancement indicates fast SF truncation

## Methods

- Deep optical spectroscopy
- Stellar population synthesis fitting
- AGN identification via emission lines
- Age and metallicity measurements
- Control sample matching

---

## Relevance to Galaxy Evolution

AGN feedback is a key mechanism for quenching massive galaxies. This work shows it leaves observable signatures in stellar populations, providing constraints on the timing and nature of quenching.
