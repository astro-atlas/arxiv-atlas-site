# arXiv:2603.09917

## The SAMI Galaxy Survey: The internal orbital structure and mass distribution of galaxies across the colour–magnitude diagram

**Authors:** J. J. Bryant, L. Cortese, J. T. Mendel, M. C. B. Ashley, S. K. Kannappan, B. Catinella, T. M. Foster, I. S. Konijn, S. P. Vaughan, S. M. Croom, et al.

**Published:** 2026-03-11

**Categories:** astro-ph.GA

**DOI:** 10.48550/arXiv.2603.09917

---

## Abstract

We present an analysis of the internal orbital structure of 2,945 galaxies from the SAMI Galaxy Survey, spanning the full colour–magnitude diagram. Using Jeans Anisotropic MGE (JAM) dynamical modelling, we derive stellar orbital distributions and mass profiles. We find that red sequence galaxies show more radially biased orbits (β ≈ 0.3) compared to blue cloud galaxies (β ≈ 0.1). The transition from rotationally supported to pressure supported systems occurs at stellar mass M* ≈ 10^10.5 M☉. These results provide constraints on the physical processes driving galaxy quenching and morphological transformation.

---

## Key Findings

- **2,945 galaxies** analyzed with JAM dynamical modelling
- **Red sequence galaxies**: more radially biased orbits (β ≈ 0.3)
- **Blue cloud galaxies**: more isotropic orbits (β ≈ 0.1)
- **Transition mass**: M* ≈ 10^10.5 M☉ marks shift from rotation to pressure support
- Orbital anisotropy correlates with galaxy colour and structural properties

---

## Methods

- SAMI Galaxy Survey integral field spectroscopy
- Jeans Anisotropic MGE (JAM) dynamical modelling
- Multi-Gaussian Expansion (MGE) for surface brightness deprojection
- Orbital anisotropy parameter β = 1 - (σ_t/σ_r)²

---

## Relevance to Galaxy Evolution

This work directly addresses the galaxy bimodality by quantifying the dynamical differences between star-forming and quiescent galaxies. The transition at M* ≈ 10^10.5 M☉ aligns with the established quenching mass scale and provides dynamical evidence for different formation pathways.
