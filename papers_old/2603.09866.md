# arXiv:2603.09866

## The Origin of the Mass-Metallicity Relation: Insights from the FIRE Simulations

**Authors:** P. F. Hopkins, A. Wetzel, D. Kereš, C. C. Hayward, C. A. Faucher-Giguère, B. D. Oppenheimer, J. S. Bullock, M. Boylan-Kolchin, T. K. Chan, E. Quataert, et al.

**Published:** 2026-03-11

**Categories:** astro-ph.GA

**DOI:** 10.48550/arXiv.2603.09866

---

## Abstract

We investigate the origin of the galaxy mass-metallicity relation (MZR) using the FIRE cosmological simulations. We find that the MZR arises from a combination of gravitational potential well depth (retaining metals) and gas accretion history (diluting metals). Low-mass galaxies have shallow potentials and high specific gas accretion rates, leading to efficient metal dilution. Massive galaxies retain metals more effectively due to deeper potentials and self-regulated star formation. The MZR evolution with redshift is primarily driven by changing gas accretion rates rather than feedback strength variations.

---

## Key Findings

- **MZR origin**: combination of potential well depth and gas accretion
- **Low-mass galaxies**: shallow potentials + high accretion → metal dilution
- **Massive galaxies**: deep potentials + self-regulated SF → metal retention
- **Redshift evolution**: driven by changing gas accretion rates
- **Feedback role**: secondary to gravitational and accretion effects

---

## Methods

- FIRE (Feedback in Realistic Environments) simulations
- Cosmological zoom-in simulations
- Metal tracking and enrichment analysis
- Gas accretion history reconstruction
- Redshift evolution comparison

---

## Relevance to Galaxy Evolution

This work provides a physical explanation for the mass-metallicity relation, showing it emerges from fundamental gravitational and gas dynamics rather than solely feedback effects.
