Title: Spectroscopic galaxy redshifts in the Peanut cluster – a massive nearly head-on cluster merger shortly after pericenter passage

ArXiv ID: 2603.10849

Authors: Natalya Lyskova, Ilfan Bikmaev, Rodion Burenin, Arina Arshinova, Eugene Churazov, Sergey Dodonov, Marat Gilfanov, Ildar Khabibullin, Irek Khamitov, Sergey Kotov, Alexey Moiseev, Sergey Sazonov, Rashid Sunyaev, Mikhail Suslikov, Roman Uklein

Abstract (short): The Peanut cluster (SRGe J023820.8+200556, z_spec~0.42) is presented as a candidate for a rare, massive merger similar to the Bullet cluster. The authors report 26 new spectroscopic redshifts measured with the 6-m BTA telescope (total 31 galaxies), finding a line-of-sight velocity distribution that may show two components separated by ~2000 km/s, though statistical tests do not conclusively require two subclusters. Assuming a single cluster, the velocity dispersion is sigma_los = 1455 +/- 83 km/s, implying M200 ~ 2e15 Msun. Combined with X-ray morphology (eROSITA/Chandra), the system is consistent with a major merger observed close to pericenter.

Relevance: galaxy clusters, cluster mergers, dynamics, mass estimates from velocity dispersion, implications for dark matter self-interaction constraints. Relevant to galaxy evolution in dense environments and cluster-scale morphology.

Key points and extracted concepts:
- New spectroscopic redshifts for 26 galaxies (31 total) in the Peanut cluster field (z~0.4095-0.4360).
- Velocity distribution shows possible two components near z~0.425 and z~0.416; line-of-sight velocity difference ~2000 km/s between brightest galaxies.
- Statistical tests (including Dressler–Schectman) do not decisively reject a single Gaussian velocity distribution; substructure evidence ambiguous.
- If single cluster: sigma_los = 1455 +/- 83 km/s -> M200 ~ 2e15 Msun (scaling relation).
- X-ray morphology from Chandra/eROSITA shows two bright substructures and spatial offset between galaxies and hot gas, consistent with near-pericenter major merger (analogy to Bullet cluster / El Gordo).
