Title: Black Hole Properties of Type-1 Active Galactic Nuclei in the North Ecliptic Pole Wide Field
Authors: Dohyeong Kim, Myungshin Im, Hyunjin Shim, Minjin Kim, et al.
arXiv ID: 2603.09976
URL: https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.09976

Summary
- Sample: 861 Type-1 AGNs in the NEP-Wide field detected in both optical and mid-infrared (MIR) surveys; redshift range z=0.09–4.71.
- Data and methods: Multi-wavelength photometry (optical to MIR) + optical spectroscopy (DESI DR1, Shim et al. 2013). SED fitting to obtain extinction-corrected MIR luminosities (rest-frame 3.4 and 4.6 μm). Emission-line fitting for C IV, Mg II, Hβ, Hα to measure FWHMs.
- Key technique: Use LMIR (monochromatic MIR luminosities) combined with BEL FWHMs as dust-insensitive BH mass and bolometric luminosity estimators (following Kim et al. 2023).
- Main results: Bolometric luminosities Lbol ≈ 10^{43.20}–10^{47.27} erg s−1; BH masses MBH ≈ 10^{7.29}–10^{9.67} M⊙; Eddington ratios ≈ 10^{−2.74}–10^{−0.08}. ~34% of optically-classified Type-1 AGNs are dust-obscured and would have underestimated Lbol without extinction correction.
- Importance: Provides a relatively extinction-free catalog of BH properties across wide z for the NEP-Wide field, useful as fiducial estimates for SPHEREx and for studies linking AGN properties to host galaxies, environment, and number densities.

Relevance: Relevant to galaxy evolution, AGN demographics, and host–AGN connection. Will be added to the ArXiv Atlas as an AGN / SMBH resource for NEP-wide multiwavelength studies.
