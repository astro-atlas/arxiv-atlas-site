Title: Star formation in the circumgalactic high-velocity cloud Complex H
ArXiv ID: 2603.10607
Authors: He et al. (2025/2026)
URL: https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.10607

Summary:
- Discovery of a binary young open cluster (Emei-1 and Emei-2) embedded in High-Velocity Cloud (HVC) Complex H (Southern Complex H, SCH).
- Clusters age: 11.2 ± 0.6 Myr.
- Distance: 13.8 ± 0.6 kpc (direct stellar distance anchor to the cloud).
- Stellar metallicity: subsolar, Z ~ 0.04–0.06 Z_sun (individual stars: 0.04^{+0.06}_{-0.02} and 0.06^{+0.07}_{-0.03} Z_sun).
- Mean systemic v_lsr ~ -180 km/s; individual measured v_lsr for two bright members: -202 ± 5 km/s and -158 ± 1 km/s.
- HI cold core (C1) peak v_lsr = -181 km/s; cold gas velocity dispersion sigma_v ~ 2 km/s; warm gas sigma_v ~ 15 km/s.
- Physical size of C1 (channel 2): ~320 × 99 pc after beam deconvolution.
- Ambient disk gas density upper limit ~ 1e-24 g cm^-3 inferred from drag modelling.
- Complex H shows a "slow-fast-slow" (SFS) latitude-velocity pattern spanning ~100 km/s over ~15 degrees.

Key findings / implications:
- Direct confirmation that star formation can occur inside HVCs / circumgalactic medium; provides first robust stellar distance and metallicity measurements for Complex H.
- Suggests HVC-cloud interactions (internal cloud-cloud collision) as a triggering mechanism for star formation in VHVCs.
- Proper motions indicate Complex H is on a prograde, south-to-north orbit interacting with the outer Galactic disk; outer layers are decelerating and merging with disk gas.
- Young stars born in HVCs can rapidly decouple from their parent gas (<~20 Myr), explaining why stellar detections in HVCs are rare.
- CO non-detections in HVCs may owe to low metallicity and dispersed molecular gas; cold HI cores may be better targets.
- Hypervelocity stars and some halo stellar structures may originate from HVC-born stars.

Data & methods highlights:
- Gaia astrometry (member selection, proper motions, parallaxes)
- LAMOST spectroscopy (spectral types, radial velocities, metallicities for brightest members)
- EBHIS 21cm HI data (channel maps, peak velocity maps, size estimates)
- Orbit integration with drag modelling to infer trajectory and ambient density constraints

Notes / open questions:
- How common is HVC star formation? Detection is biased toward extremely young (<20 Myr) systems.
- Fate of clusters: tidal dissolution and eventual detachment from gas; potential contribution to halo stellar populations.
- Need for deep CO/other molecular tracers targeted on cold HI cores.
