# arXiv:2603.09739

## The Stellar Mass Assembly of Brightest Cluster Galaxies

**Authors:** B. P. Venemans, H. J. A. Röttgering, G. K. Miley, J. D. Kurk, F. Walter, A. C. Fabian, R. G. McMahon, L. Pentericci, W. van Breugel, C. L. Carilli, et al.

**Published:** 2026-03-11

**Categories:** astro-ph.GA

**DOI:** 10.48550/arXiv.2603.09739

---

## Abstract

We trace the stellar mass assembly of brightest cluster galaxies (BCGs) from z~3 to z~0 using a sample of 450 BCGs from the Spitzer/IRAC BCG Survey. We find that BCGs grow in stellar mass by a factor of ~4 since z~1, with the growth rate declining with time. The mass assembly is dominated by dry mergers at z<1, while star formation contributes significantly at z>1. The most massive BCGs (M*>10^12 M☉) show evidence for accelerated growth, possibly due to enhanced merger rates in the most massive clusters.

---

## Key Findings

- **450 BCGs** from Spitzer/IRAC survey
- **Mass growth**: factor of ~4 since z~1
- **Declining growth rate** with cosmic time
- **z<1**: dry mergers dominate assembly
- **z>1**: star formation contributes significantly
- **Most massive BCGs**: accelerated growth, enhanced merger rates

---

## Methods

- Spitzer/IRAC photometry
- Stellar mass estimates from SED fitting
- Merger rate calculations
- Star formation history reconstruction
- Redshift evolution analysis

---

## Relevance to Galaxy Evolution

BCGs are the most massive galaxies in the universe. Understanding their assembly history provides constraints on the extreme end of galaxy formation and the role of mergers in building massive galaxies.
