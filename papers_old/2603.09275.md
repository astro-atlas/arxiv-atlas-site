# arXiv:2603.09275

## The Role of Cold Gas Streams in Fueling Galaxy Growth

**Authors:** D. Kereš, N. Katz, D. H. Weinberg, R. Davé

**Published:** 2026-03-11

**Categories:** astro-ph.GA

**DOI:** 10.48550/arXiv.2603.09275

---

## Abstract

We use cosmological hydrodynamical simulations to study the role of cold gas streams in fueling galaxy growth at z~2-4. We find that cold streams (T < 10^5 K) deliver gas directly to galaxy centers, bypassing the hot halo and maintaining high star formation rates. The stream accretion rate correlates with dark matter halo mass as Ṁ_stream ∝ M_halo^1.2. At z~2, streams contribute ~70% of the gas accreted by massive galaxies (M_halo > 10^12 M☉), declining to ~30% by z~1 as virial shocks become more stable.

---

## Key Findings

- **Cold streams** (T < 10^5 K) fuel galaxy growth at z~2-4
- **Direct delivery**: gas reaches galaxy centers, bypassing hot halo
- **Accretion rate**: Ṁ_stream ∝ M_halo^1.2
- **z~2 contribution**: ~70% of gas accretion for massive galaxies
- **z~1 contribution**: ~30% (declining as shocks stabilize)

## Methods

- Cosmological hydrodynamical simulations
- Cold stream identification and tracking
- Accretion rate calculations
- Redshift evolution analysis

## Relevance to Galaxy Evolution

Cold streams provide an efficient channel for gas accretion that sustains high star formation rates at high redshift. Their decline contributes to the cosmic decline in star formation activity.
