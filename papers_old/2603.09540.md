## The Evolution of the Galaxy Stellar Mass Function in COSMOS

**Authors:** P. Capak, O. Ilbert, H. J. McCracken, M. Salvato, D. B. Sanders, N. Scoville, J. S. Kartaltepe, S. Arnouts, E. Le Floc'h, A. M. Koekemoer, et al.

**Published:** 2026-03-11

**Categories:** astro-ph.GA

## Abstract

We present measurements of the galaxy stellar mass function (GSMF) in the COSMOS field from z~0.2 to z~3. We find that the characteristic mass M* increases with redshift as log M* ∝ 0.2(1+z), while the faint-end slope α remains roughly constant at α ≈ -1.3. The integrated stellar mass density increases by a factor of ~10 from z~3 to z~0, with the growth rate declining at z<1. Our results are consistent with a hierarchical assembly scenario where massive galaxies form through the accretion of smaller systems.

## Key Findings

- **GSMF measured** from z~0.2 to z~3 in COSMOS
- **Characteristic mass**: log M* ∝ 0.2(1+z)
- **Faint-end slope**: α ≈ -1.3 (constant with redshift)
- **Stellar mass density**: increases by factor of ~10 from z~3 to z~0
- **Declining growth** at z<1
- **Hierarchical assembly**: consistent with model

## Methods

- COSMOS photometric redshifts
- Stellar mass estimates from SED fitting
- Maximum likelihood GSMF fitting
- Schechter function parameter evolution
- Mass density integration

The GSMF evolution provides fundamental constraints on galaxy formation models, showing how mass is assembled across cosmic time in a hierarchical universe.
