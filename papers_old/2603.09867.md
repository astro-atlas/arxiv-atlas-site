# arXiv:2603.09867

## The Morphological Transformation of Galaxies in the IllustrisTNG Simulation

**Authors:** D. Nelson, A. Pillepich, V. Springel, R. Weinberger, L. Hernquist, M. Vogelsberger, S. Genel, P. Torrey, F. Marinacci, J. Naiman, et al.

**Published:** 2026-03-11

**Categories:** astro-ph.GA

**DOI:** 10.48550/arXiv.2603.09867

---

## Abstract

We analyze the morphological transformation of galaxies in the IllustrisTNG simulation, tracking 10,000 galaxies from z=4 to z=0. We find that major mergers (mass ratio >0.25) are responsible for 60% of elliptical galaxy formation, while minor mergers and secular processes contribute 25% and 15% respectively. The morphological mix evolves from 80% disk-dominated at z~2 to 50% at z~0, with the transition accelerating at z<1. AGN feedback plays a critical role in maintaining quiescence in massive ellipticals by preventing gas cooling and disk regrowth.

## Key Findings

- **10,000 galaxies** tracked from z=4 to z=0
- **Major mergers**: responsible for 60% of elliptical formation
- **Minor mergers**: contribute 25% to morphological transformation
- **Secular processes**: account for 15% of transformation
- **Morphological evolution**: 80% disks at z~2 → 50% at z~0
- **AGN feedback**: critical for maintaining quiescence in massive ellipticals
