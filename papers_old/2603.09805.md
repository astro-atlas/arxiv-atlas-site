# arXiv:2603.09805

## The Impact of Environment on the Tully-Fisher Relation

**Authors:** A. J. Ponomareva, S. S. McGaugh, F. Lelli, S. Schombert, E. Papastergis

**Published:** 2026-03-11

**Categories:** astro-ph.GA

**DOI:** 10.48550/arXiv.2603.09805

---

## Abstract

We examine how environment affects the Tully-Fisher relation (TFR) using a sample of 800 spiral galaxies spanning field, group, and cluster environments. We find that cluster galaxies have systematically lower rotation velocities at fixed stellar mass (Δlog V_rot ≈ -0.08 dex) compared to field galaxies. This offset is consistent with tidal stripping of dark matter halos in dense environments. The TFR zero-point evolution with redshift is also environment-dependent, with cluster galaxies showing stronger evolution than field galaxies.

---

## Key Findings

- **800 spiral galaxies** across field, group, and cluster environments
- **Cluster offset**: Δlog V_rot ≈ -0.08 dex at fixed mass
- **Tidal stripping**: dark matter halos affected in dense environments
- **Redshift evolution**: environment-dependent TFR zero-point evolution
- **Stronger evolution**: in cluster galaxies vs. field

## Methods

- HI line width measurements
- Stellar mass estimates from SED fitting
- Environment classification (field/group/cluster)
- Tully-Fisher relation fitting
- Redshift evolution analysis

---

## Relevance to Galaxy Evolution

The TFR is a fundamental scaling relation. Environmental effects on the TFR provide constraints on how galaxy halos are modified by interactions and tidal stripping.
