## The Chemical Enrichment of the Circumgalactic Medium

**Authors:** N. B. H. Tejos, J. N. Prochaska, J. X. Prochaska, J. F. Hennawi, S. Lopez, J. Tumlinson, M. S. Peeples, J. M. O'Meara, T. M. Tripp, A. J. Fox, et al.

**Published:** 2026-03-11

**Categories:** astro-ph.GA

**DOI:** 10.48550/arXiv.2603.09305

## Abstract

We present a comprehensive study of chemical enrichment in the circumgalactic medium (CGM) using 500 quasar sightlines probing galaxies at z~0.1-0.5. We find that the CGM metallicity increases with galaxy stellar mass as Z_CGM ∝ M*^0.3, reaching ~0.3 Z☉ for M* ~ 10^11 M☉. The metallicity gradient extends to ~100 kpc from galaxies, with higher ionization gas showing higher metallicities. We interpret this as evidence for metal-rich outflows from star-forming regions mixing with pristine accreted gas in the CGM.

---

## Key Findings

- **500 quasar sightlines** probing CGM at z~0.1-0.5
- **CGM metallicity**: Z_CGM ∝ M*^0.3
- **Maximum metallicity**: ~0.3 Z☉ for M* ~ 10^11 M☉
- **Metallicity gradient**: extends to ~100 kpc
- **Ionization dependence**: higher ionization gas has higher metallicity
- **Outflow+accretion**: metal-rich outflows mix with pristine gas

---

## Methods

- Quasar absorption line spectroscopy
- Galaxy-CGM association via impact parameter
- Metallicity measurements from ionization modeling
- Mass-metallicity relation fitting
- Ionization state analysis

---

## Relevance to Galaxy Evolution

The CGM is a critical interface between galaxies and the intergalactic medium. Understanding its chemical enrichment provides constraints on feedback, gas cycling, and metal transport in galaxy formation.
