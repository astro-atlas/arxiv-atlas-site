## The ALMA Spectroscopic Survey in the Hubble Ultra Deep Field: The Molecular Gas Content of Galaxies at z=0.5-3

**Authors:** R. Decarli, M. Aravena, M. Bethermin, A. C. Carniani, F. Walter, P. Cox, D. Riechers, I. Smail, A. Weiss, F. Bertoldi, et al.

**Published:** 2026-03-11

**Categories:** astro-ph.GA

**DOI:** 10.48550/arXiv.2603.09873

---

## Abstract

We present ALMA observations of CO(3-2) and CO(4-3) emission in the Hubble Ultra Deep Field, detecting molecular gas in 156 galaxies at z=0.5-3. We find that the molecular gas fraction (f_gas = M_mol/M*) increases with redshift as f_gas ∝ (1+z)^1.8, reaching ~50% at z~2. The depletion time (t_dep = M_mol/SFR) remains roughly constant at ~1 Gyr across the redshift range, indicating that star formation efficiency per unit gas mass does not evolve strongly. These results suggest that the elevated SFRs at high redshift are primarily driven by larger gas reservoirs rather than enhanced star formation efficiency.

---

## Key Findings

- **156 galaxies** detected in CO(3-2) and CO(4-3) at z=0.5-3
- **Molecular gas fraction**: f_gas ∝ (1+z)^1.8, reaching ~50% at z~2
- **Depletion time**: t_dep ~ 1 Gyr (constant with redshift)
- **Star formation efficiency**: does not evolve strongly
- **High-z SFRs driven by**: larger gas reservoirs, not enhanced efficiency

## Methods

- ALMA CO(3-2) and CO(4-3) observations
- Hubble Ultra Deep Field sample
- Molecular gas mass calculations
- Depletion time and gas fraction analysis
- Redshift evolution modeling

---

## Relevance to Galaxy Evolution

This work provides direct evidence for the gas-driven nature of high-redshift star formation. The constant depletion time suggests star formation physics remains similar across cosmic time, with cosmic star formation history driven by gas availability.
