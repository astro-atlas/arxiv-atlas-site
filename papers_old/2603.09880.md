# arXiv:2603.09880

## The Evolution of Galaxy Clustering in the COSMOS Field: A Machine Learning Approach

**Authors:** M. Salvato, O. Ilbert, C. Laigle, H. J. McCracken, P. Capak, D. Masters, M. Aubert, S. Fotopoulou, C. Steinhardt, J. S. Kartaltepe, et al.

**Published:** 2026-03-11

**Categories:** astro-ph.GA

**DOI:** 10.48550/arXiv.2603.09880

---

## Abstract

We present a machine learning analysis of galaxy clustering evolution in the COSMOS field spanning 0 < z < 3. Using a random forest classifier trained on photometric and morphological features, we identify galaxy environments (field, group, cluster) with 85% accuracy. We find that the clustering amplitude increases with redshift as (1+z)^(-1.2), consistent with hierarchical structure formation. The fraction of quiescent galaxies in dense environments increases from 20% at z~2 to 60% at z~0, indicating strong environmental quenching.

---

## Key Findings

- **Machine learning classification** of galaxy environments (85% accuracy)
- **Clustering amplitude evolution**: follows (1+z)^(-1.2)
- **Consistent with hierarchical** structure formation
- **Environmental quenching**: quiescent fraction increases from 20% (z~2) to 60% (z~0)
- **Dense environment fraction** evolves with cosmic time

---

## Methods

- Random forest classifier
- COSMOS photometric redshifts (0 < z < 3)
- Morphological parameters from HST imaging
- Two-point correlation function analysis
- Environment classification (field/group/cluster)

---

## Relevance to Galaxy Evolution

This work quantifies the role of environment in galaxy evolution, showing that dense environments accelerate quenching. The evolution of clustering provides constraints on cosmological models and galaxy formation scenarios.
