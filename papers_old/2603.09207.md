# arXiv:2603.09207

## The Formation of Bulges in Disk Galaxies: Clues from the Local Universe

**Authors:** J. G. Moreno, P. Erwin, R. F. Peletier, R. J. McDermid, L. Coccato, M. Sarzi, L. M. Young, E. M. Corsini, E. Dalla Bontà, L. K. Hunt, et al.

**Published:** 2026-03-11

**Categories:** astro-ph.GA

**DOI:** 10.48550/arXiv.2603.09207

## Abstract

We study bulge formation in 800 local disk galaxies using a combination of photometric decomposition and stellar population synthesis. We find that pseudobulges (formed via secular processes) comprise 60% of bulges in late-type spirals, while classical bulges (formed via mergers) dominate in early-types. The transition from pseudobulge to classical bulge dominance occurs at Hubble type Sbc. Bulge-to-total ratios correlate with structural parameters: pseudobulges show lower B/T and more disk-like properties, while classical bulges follow the same scaling relations as elliptical galaxies.

---

## Key Findings

- **800 local disk galaxies** analyzed
- **Pseudobulges**: 60% of bulges in late-type spirals
- **Classical bulges**: dominate in early-types
- **Transition**: at Hubble type Sbc
- **B/T correlations**: pseudobulges have lower B/T, disk-like properties
- **Classical bulges**: follow elliptical galaxy scaling relations

## Methods

- Photometric bulge-disk decomposition
- Stellar population synthesis
- Bulge classification (pseudobulge vs. classical)
- Structural parameter analysis
- Scaling relation comparison

---

## Relevance to Galaxy Evolution

Bulge formation is a key aspect of disk galaxy evolution. This work shows that both secular processes and mergers contribute, with their relative importance depending on galaxy type.
