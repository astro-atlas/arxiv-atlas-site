# arXiv:2603.09828

## The Role of Bars in Driving Central Star Formation in Disk Galaxies

**Authors:** S. H. K. Kim, R. J. Buta, A. Li, J. H. Knapen, D. B. Sanders, J. M. Mazzarella, S. Laine, E. Laurikainen, H. Salo

**Published:** 2026-03-11

**Categories:** astro-ph.GA

**DOI:** 10.48550/arXiv.2603.09828

---

## Abstract

We investigate how stellar bars affect central star formation in 2,500 disk galaxies from the SDSS. Using Hα and UV imaging, we find that barred galaxies show enhanced central star formation rates (SFR) by a factor of 1.5-2 compared to unbarred galaxies of similar mass. The bar-driven inflow of gas to the center creates a reservoir for star formation, with the effect strongest in intermediate-mass galaxies (M* ~ 10^10-10^11 M☉). The central SFR enhancement correlates with bar strength as measured by the bar ellipticity.

---

## Key Findings

- **2,500 disk galaxies** analyzed from SDSS
- **Barred galaxies**: 1.5-2× higher central SFR than unbarred
- **Gas inflow**: bars drive gas to galaxy centers
- **Strongest effect**: intermediate-mass galaxies (M* ~ 10^10-10^11 M☉)
- **Correlation**: central SFR enhancement ∝ bar strength (ellipticity)
- **Reservoir creation**: bars build central gas reservoirs

---

## Methods

- SDSS imaging and spectroscopy
- Hα and UV star formation indicators
- Bar identification and strength measurement
- Central SFR analysis
- Mass-matched comparison of barred vs. unbarred

---

## Relevance to Galaxy Evolution

Bars are important drivers of secular evolution in disk galaxies, redistributing angular momentum and gas to fuel central star formation and potentially build bulges.
