# arXiv:2603.09519

## The Impact of Mergers on Galaxy Sizes and Structural Parameters

**Authors:** S. Kaviraj, C. J. Conselice, J. S. Dunlop, R. S. Ellis, R. J. McLure, M. Cirasuolo, A. M. Koekemoer, O. Almaini, E. R. Stanway, A. Mortlock, et al.

**Published:** 2026-03-11

**Categories:** astro-ph.GA

**DOI:** 10.48550/arXiv.2603.09519

---

## Abstract

We study the impact of galaxy mergers on structural parameters using a sample of 1,200 merging systems from the CANDELS survey. We find that major mergers increase galaxy sizes by 30-50% and Sérsic indices by Δn ≈ 0.5-1.0. The size increase is most pronounced in gas-rich mergers, while gas-poor mergers show more modest changes. Post-merger galaxies require ~1-2 Gyr to relax to equilibrium configurations, during which they show elevated Sérsic indices and disturbed morphologies.

---

## Key Findings

- **1,200 merging systems** from CANDELS
- **Size increase**: 30-50% from major mergers
- **Sérsic index increase**: Δn ≈ 0.5-1.0
- **Gas-rich mergers**: more pronounced size increase
- **Relaxation time**: ~1-2 Gyr to equilibrium
- **Elevated Sérsic indices** during relaxation

---

## Methods

- CANDELS HST imaging
- Structural parameter fitting (GALFIT)
- Merger identification via morphological asymmetry
- Size and Sérsic index evolution tracking
- Gas fraction estimates from SED fitting

---

## Relevance to Galaxy Evolution

Mergers are key drivers of structural transformation. This work quantifies how mergers modify galaxy sizes and shapes, providing constraints on merger-driven evolution scenarios.
