# arXiv:2603.09340

## The Star Formation Main Sequence at z~4-6: Evidence for a Turnover?

**Authors:** R. S. Somerville, P. Behroozi, R. C. W. Houghton, C. C. Williams, P. G. van Dokkum, A. van der Wel, M. Franx, I. Labbé, G. Brammer, V. A. Villanueva, et al.

**Published:** 2026-03-11

**Categories:** astro-ph.GA

**DOI:** 10.48550/arXiv.2603.09340

---

## Abstract

We investigate the star formation main sequence (SFMS) at z~4-6 using deep HST and JWST imaging in the HUDF and GOODS fields. We find evidence for a turnover in the SFMS at M* ≈ 10^10.5 M☉, where the SFR-mass relation flattens and potentially declines at higher masses. This turnover is consistent with the onset of quenching in massive galaxies at early cosmic times. The SFMS normalization increases with redshift as (1+z)^2.5, but the slope and turnover mass remain roughly constant.

---

## Key Findings

- **SFMS at z~4-6** from HST+JWST deep fields
- **Turnover evidence**: at M* ≈ 10^10.5 M☉
- **Flattening/decline**: SFR-mass relation at high masses
- **Early quenching**: onset in massive galaxies
- **Normalization evolution**: ∝ (1+z)^2.5
- **Constant slope and turnover mass**

## Methods

- HST and JWST photometry
- Photometric redshifts
- SED fitting for stellar masses and SFRs
- SFMS fitting and turnover detection
- Redshift evolution analysis

---

## Relevance to Galaxy Evolution

The SFMS turnover at high redshift suggests massive galaxies begin quenching earlier than previously thought, providing constraints on the timing and mechanisms of galaxy quenching.
