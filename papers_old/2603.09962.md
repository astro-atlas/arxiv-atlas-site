## Tidal Features in Galaxy Pairs: A Deep Learning Approach

**Authors:** S. K. Pandey, A. K. Singhal, S. Paul, S. Gupta, S. Sharma, S. K. Pandey

**Published:** 2026-03-11

**Categories:** astro-ph.GA

**DOI:** 10.48550/arXiv.2603.09962

---

## Abstract

We present a deep learning framework for detecting tidal features in galaxy pairs using the TidalNet architecture. Our model achieves 94% accuracy in identifying tidal tails, bridges, and shells in SDSS and DESI Legacy Survey imaging. We analyze 2,847 galaxy pairs from the Galaxy Zoo catalog, finding tidal features in 23% of systems. The detection rate increases with pair separation and stellar mass ratio. This work demonstrates the potential of deep learning for systematic studies of galaxy interactions and their role in morphological transformation.

---

## Key Findings

- **TidalNet architecture** achieves 94% accuracy on feature detection
- **23% of galaxy pairs** show detectable tidal features
- Detection rate correlates with:
  - Pair separation (closer pairs show more features)
  - Stellar mass ratio (more equal-mass pairs show more features)
- Features identified: tidal tails, bridges, shells, and asymmetric structures

---

## Methods

- Deep learning with TidalNet convolutional neural network
- Training on SDSS and DESI Legacy Survey imaging
- Galaxy Zoo catalog as reference dataset
- 2,847 galaxy pairs analyzed

---

## Relevance to Galaxy Evolution

This work provides a scalable method for identifying galaxy interactions, which are key drivers of morphological transformation. The high detection rate (23%) suggests tidal interactions are common in the local universe and contribute significantly to galaxy evolution.
