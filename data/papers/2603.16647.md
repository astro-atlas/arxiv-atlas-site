# Metadata

* **Title:** Particle acceleration at recollimation shocks in sub-relativistic jets
* **Authors:** E. Peretti, E. Amato, S. S. Cerri, G. Morlino, L.P. Pullano, S. Recchia
* **arXiv ID:** 2603.16647
* **DOI:** (not provided in source)
* **Submitted date:** 2026-03-18
* **Abstract:** Growing observational evidence suggests that sub-relativistic astrophysical jets may accelerate particles at slowly evolving standing shocks. Recollimation shocks are expected to develop when jets expand in dense environments; their formation may be mediated by the pressure of the cocoon surrounding the jet, while remaining compatible with a quasi-stationary behavior. Despite their high inclination relative to the jet axis, such shocks can be strong and enable efficient particle acceleration. The aim of this work is to improve the general understanding of particle acceleration via diffusive shock acceleration at recollimation shocks by developing a versatile modeling framework applicable to different classes of astrophysical jets, including Seyfert galaxies, microquasars, and protostellar systems. The authors extend an analytic jet hydrodynamics model to the sub-relativistic regime, identify recollimation-shock locations, and solve a spatially-dependent transport equation to predict particle spectra and maximum energies.

5) Section 4: Results
- Key claims/findings (3-5):
  - Using prototype parameters for Seyfert (SEY_0), microquasar (MQ_0), and protostellar (PS_0) jets, the model predicts maximum proton energies ranging from few GeV (PS, Kolmogorov) up to EeV (SEY, Bohm optimistic).
  - Diffusion regime strongly affects the cutoff shape and E_max: Bohm yields highest E_max; Kraichnan chosen as benchmark; Kolmogorov typically gives lowest E_max.
  - Upstream confinement (diffusion length ~ z_sh) usually sets E_max, but rapidly decaying downstream turbulence can make downstream escape limit E_max.
  - Parameter scans show E_max scales ~ dot{M}^{1/2} and has a dependence on u_0 that depends on diffusion index (Bohm vs Kraichnan); E_max also scales inversely with opening angle theta_0.
- Key concepts: prototype source parameters, Hillas-like estimate, turbulence decay, upstream vs downstream-limited confinement, escape into cocoon and hadronic signatures.

6) Section 5: Discussion
- Key claims/findings (3-5):
  - The semi-analytic 1D model captures leading-order DSA physics at recollimation shocks but omits multi-dimensional transport, ordered guide fields, full obliquity physics, and time-dependence; these omissions are acknowledged and avenues for future work are given.
  - The model is applicable to sub-relativistic / mildly relativistic jets; relativistic generalization required for fast AGN jets/blazars.
  - Downstream turbulence decay, lateral escape, and the microphysics at the jet head are identified as important uncertainties affecting particle escape and possible hadronic signatures in the cocoon.
- Key concepts: model limitations, oblique-shock physics, guide-field effects, multi-dimensional transport, relativistic regimes.
