# Faraday Rotation Measure of 3C 84 and the Circumnuclear Environment of NGC 1275

## Metadata

* **Title**: Faraday Rotation Measure of 3C 84 and the Circumnuclear Environment of NGC 1275
* **Authors**: Jongho Park, Minchul Kam, Kunwoo Yi, Ilje Cho, Sang-Sung Lee, Do-Young Byun, Jeffrey A. Hodgson, Kiyoaki Wajima, Motoki Kino, Kazuhiro Hada, Kazuya Takahashi, Shoko Koyama, Yunjeong Lee, Guang-Yao Zhao, Junghwan Oh, Hyunwook Ro, Yongjun Chen, Taehyun Jung, Jae-Young Kim, Thomas P. * **DOI**: (not yet assigned at submission; submitted 2026-03-21)
* **Submitted date**: 2026-03-21
* **Comments**: Submitted to ApJ; 36 pages, 21 figures
* **Abstract**: We present multi-frequency polarimetric observations of 3C 84 (NGC 1275) using the Korean VLBI Network (KVN), the Very Long Baseline Array (VLBA), and the High Sensitivity Array (HSA) from 2015 to 2024. The observations span 8 to 141 GHz and resolve the parsec-scale jet structure. We detect linear polarization from multiple inner jet components (C4, C3, and K) spanning 3–7 mas from the black hole, and from the southern lobe at ~15 mas. The rotation measure (RM) toward all these components decreases monotonically with distance from the black hole, following a single power-law relation RM ∝ r^(−2.7±0.2) spanning over a decade in spatial scale. We show that this unified RM–distance relation is naturally explained by a common circumnuclear ambient medium within the Bondi radius (~11.8 pc) of NGC 1275. Modeling the RM profile, we constrain the electron density (n_e ∝ r^(−1.5 to −2)) and magnetic-field strength (B ∝ r^(−1.7 to −2.2)) within the Bondi radius, yielding B = 0.1–1.5 μG at 11.8 pc and rapidly increasing inward. This provides the first spatially resolved measurement of the ambient magnetic-field strength within the parsec-scale Bondi radius of an elliptical galaxy.

## Contents

### Introduction

#### Key claims
1. 3C 84 (the radio source in NGC 1275/Perseus A) has diverse RM measurements at different frequencies and spatial scales that have been impossible to reconcile — this paper provides the first unified explanation.
2. Previous detections at radio frequencies (0.5–2% polarization limits at 81–348 GHz with IRAM PdBI) placed strong upper limits, requiring RM dispersion ≥ 10^6 rad/m^2 to suppress polarization even at 354 GHz.
3. CARMA and SMA detected 1–3% linear polarization at 1.3 mm during 2011–2013, with average RM of 8.7×10^5 rad/m^2 attributed to within 0.4 pc of the black hole.
4. VLBA 43 GHz measurements (2015–2016) found RM of (3–6)×10^5 rad/m^2 at the termination region C3.
5. The paper shows for the first time that all observed RM values can be consistently explained by a single physical origin: the circumnuclear ambient medium of the host galaxy NGC 1275.

### Section 2: Observations and Data Reduction

#### Key claims
1. KVN simultaneous multi-frequency capability (43–141 GHz) is uniquely suited to measure large RM values because it enables quasi-simultaneous multi-frequency polarimetric monitoring and can resolve nπ ambiguity with narrow frequency spacing.
2. An upgraded 16-Gbps OCTAD-based recording system (post-2022) enabled simultaneous 4-band observations (43, 44, 45.5, 46.5, 86.2, 88, 91, 93 GHz) in a single session, greatly improving RM measurement reliability.
3. The D-term (instrumental polarization) calibration used the GPCAL pipeline, yielding significantly better accuracy than standard AIPS LPCAL by using multiple calibrators without the similarity approximation.
4. 18 epochs of VLBA BU data (2015 Dec–2017 May) were analyzed; 3C 84 is monitored monthly, with 20–30 min on-source time per epoch at 43 GHz.
5. HSA 8 GHz data (2023 December) included all ten VLBA stations, phased VLA, and Effelsberg, providing superb sensitivity for the southern lobe polarization.

### Section 3: Results

#### Key claims
1. Polarized emission from C3 was consistently detected with VLBA 43 GHz from 2015 December to 2017 May, after the emergence of a bright hotspot; after correcting systematic EVPA calibration errors using comparison sources, the average RM at C3 is (3.9±0.6)×10^5 rad/m^2.
2. After 2017 May, the VLBA no longer reliably detected C3 polarization, but the KVN consistently detected it at 43–141 GHz (2018 Feb–2024 Dec), with RM values ranging from 1.0 to 3.6×10^5 rad/m^2 and fractional polarization up to 15–20% at higher frequencies.
3. A new polarized component C4 (upstream of C3, ~3 mas from core) emerged robustly in 2022 December, with RM values systematically larger (2.6–3.5×10^5 rad/m^2) than C3 in the same epochs; an even higher RM subcomponent C4a was detected in 2024 November at (6.1±1.1)×10^5 rad/m^2.
4. Component K (downstream of C3, 5–7 mas) showed the smallest RM values (0.5–0.9×10^5 rad/m^2) whenever detected; the consistent order C4 > C3 > K in the same epochs establishes a clear distance-dependent RM gradient.
5. HSA 8 GHz detected linear polarization at the southern lobe (15 mas from core) with RM of (0.4±0.5)×10^4 rad/m^2, consistent with the earlier Taylor (2006) VLBA measurement of 7000 rad/m^2 at the same location 20 years prior.

### Section 4: Discussion

#### Key claims
1. **External Faraday rotation confirmed**: EVPA rotations exceeding 45° with good λ^2 fit (e.g., >150° rotation between 86 and 141 GHz in 2018 February) conclusively indicate the Faraday screen is external to the jet; internal Faraday rotation would deviate from λ^2 for rotations above ~45°.
2. **RM–distance power law RM ∝ r^(−2.7±0.2)**: All RM measurements from 1–15 pc from the black hole, spanning multiple instruments, multiple decades, and multiple spatial scales, follow a single power-law relation. This is much steeper than M87 (k~1).
3. **Circumnuclear ambient medium is the Faraday screen**: The medium within the Bondi radius (11.8 pc) provides a coherent external Faraday screen. RIAF, jet boundary layer, and individual gas cloud scenarios are all disfavored by the spatial extent, positive-only RM sign, and the persistence of RM after hotspot disruption.
4. **Electron density and magnetic field profiles constrained**: For n_e ∝ r^(−1.5 to −2), the RM profile implies B ∝ r^(−1.7 to −2.2) within the Bondi radius; at the Bondi radius, B = 0.1–1.5 μG and increases rapidly inward toward mG–G near the VLBI core.
5. **First spatially resolved ambient magnetic field measurement in an elliptical galaxy**: Previous measurements relied on theoretical estimates or galaxy-scale RM averages; this work provides parsec-scale resolution within the Bondi radius, first measurement of its kind.

## Critical Analysis

### 1. Key claims and findings (3–5)

1. **Unified RM–distance power law RM ∝ r^(−2.7±0.2)**: The most striking result is that all RM measurements of 3C 84 from 1–15 pc — spanning the inner jet components C4a, C4, C3, K, and the southern lobe — follow a single power-law relation across multiple instruments, multiple decades (2004–2024), and a decade of spatial scale. This is demonstrated with Pearson r = −0.876, p = 2.1×10^-9. The unification of RM measurements that had previously seemed discrepant is the central achievement.

2. **Circumnuclear ambient medium as the Faraday screen**: The paper provides the first self-consistent physical interpretation in which the entire jet of 3C 84 is embedded in a common circumnuclear ambient medium filling the Bondi sphere (r < 11.8 pc). This rules out prior hypotheses of RIAF, jet boundary layer, or individual gas cloud origins through three lines of argument: (a) persistent RM after hotspot disruption; (b) spatially extended RM across multiple components; (c) consistently positive RM sign indicating uniform field direction over parsec scales.

3. **First spatially resolved ambient magnetic field in an elliptical galaxy**: By combining the RM profile with electron density models, the paper constrains B ∝ r^(−1.7 to −2.2) within the Bondi radius, giving B = 0.1–1.5 μG at 11.8 pc increasing to ~1–20 G near the VLBI core. Previous elliptical galaxy magnetic field measurements were galaxy-averaged or theoretical.

4. **First detections of polarization from C4 and K**: These new detections at multiple epochs confirm the external Faraday screen model and show the RM gradient is a persistent, coherent spatial structure, not an isolated or transient feature.

5. **RIAF disfavored on multiple grounds**: The paper argues that a RIAF cannot explain the parsec-scale RM because (a) the Eddington ratio (~0.04–0.09%) places 3C 84 near but not deep in the RIAF regime; (b) the coexistence of BLR emission lines and FFA disk absorption requires cold (T~10^4 K) gas that would be destroyed if a RIAF extended to parsec scales; (c) the RM profile slope (k=2.7) is far steeper than RIAF model predictions.

### 2. Papers/concepts this paper supports

- **Burn 1966** (10.1093/mnras/133.1.67): The external Faraday rotation criterion (EVPA rotation > 45° with good λ^2 fit) is confirmed observationally.
- **Taylor 2006** (10.1111/j.1365-2966.2006.10244.x): The RM at the southern lobe (~7000 rad/m^2) is confirmed 20 years later and shown to lie on the same power-law, supporting long-term stability of the Faraday screen.
- **Trippe 2012** suggestion: That the circumnuclear ambient medium is the origin of Faraday rotation is now confirmed with spatially resolved VLBI data.
- **Bondi accretion theory** (Bondi 1952): The free-fall density profile (n_e ∝ r^-1.5) within the Bondi radius is a consistent model for the electron density; B ∝ r^-2 is consistent with a radial-dominated magnetic field in infalling gas.
- **Fabian et al. 2006** ICM density profile: The kpc-scale electron density (n_e ∝ r^-0.67) connecting smoothly inward provides the outer boundary condition for the model.

### 3. Papers/concepts this paper contradicts or challenges

- **Plambeck 2014** (10.1088/0004-637X/797/1/66): Their assumption that the CARMA/SMA RM comes from within 0.4 pc of the core (attributed to a RIAF or flattened disk) is challenged. This paper argues the polarized emission was dominated by the unresolved C3 component, not the core. The RIAF as primary Faraday screen is disfavored.
- **Kim 2019** (10.1051/0004-6361/201832920): The jet boundary layer as the dominant Faraday screen is disfavored because (a) large RM values > 10^5 rad/m^2 are unusual for boundary layers in nearby AGN; (b) the consistently positive RM sign (no temporal sign reversals) contrasts with behavior expected for dynamical jet sheaths; (c) a common radial trend across all jet components is inconsistent with a localized sheath.
- **Paraschos 2024 (EHT)** core RM estimate of 6×10^6 rad/m^2: This estimate may be unreliable because it assumes unresolved ALMA polarization originates at the core, whereas this paper shows polarization is consistently dominated by downstream jet components.
- **Common assumption** that 3C 84 core polarization is detectable: This paper concludes that weak core polarization features in VLBA/KVN images are residual instrumental artifacts, not real detections — a significant reinterpretation.
- **Previously quoted Eddington ratio** of 0.4% from Plambeck 2014: This paper argues the true value is ~0.04–0.09%, reducing it by 4–10× through better SED assumptions and black hole mass.

Cite Laing 1980 (10.1093/mnras/193.3.439) and this paper.
- **jet-cocoon-dynamics.astro** (existing): Add that jet components embedded in circumnuclear ambient media probe the density and magnetic-field structure of the medium through RM measurements. The 3C 84 case provides a template for linking jet kinematics to ambient medium properties.
