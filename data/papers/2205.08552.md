# 2205.08552: A trail of dark matter-free galaxies from a bullet dwarf collision

**Authors:** Pieter van Dokkum, Zili Shen, Michael A. Keim, Sebastian Trujillo-Gomez, Shany Danieli, Dhruba Dutta Chowdhury, Roberto Abraham, Charlie Conroy, J. M. Diederik Kruijssen, Daisuke Nagai, Aaron Romanowsky
**arXiv ID:** 2205.08552
**DOI:** 10.1038/s41586-022-04665-6
**Category:** astro-ph.GA

## Abstract

The paper argues that the ultra-diffuse galaxies NGC1052-DF2 and NGC1052-DF4 formed together in a high-velocity, gas-rich collision between dwarf galaxies (a "bullet dwarf" collision). Gas separated from dark matter during the impact and formed star clusters/galaxies largely free of dark matter; the authors identify a linear trail of candidate dark-matter-poor objects and predict dark-matter-dominated remnants at the leading edges.
