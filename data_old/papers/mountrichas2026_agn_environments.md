# Large-scale environments of star-forming active galactic nuclei: How black hole mass, accretion rate, and luminosity connect to dark matter halos

**Authors:** G. Mountrichas, F. J. Carrera, F. Shankar, A. Georgakakis

**arXiv ID:** 2603.11169

**Date:** 2026-03-11

**Status:** A&A accepted (v1)

## Abstract Summary

Mountrichas et al. combine X-ray AGN samples from the XXL and Stripe 82X surveys to study how large-scale environment (dark-matter halo mass) correlates with black-hole properties: black-hole mass (M_BH), Eddington ratio (λ_Edd), and X-ray luminosity (L_X). The authors build a sample of 427 broad-line AGN at 0.5 < z < 1.2 and >20,000 galaxies, derive host-galaxy properties with a consistent SED-fitting pipeline, infer dark-matter halo (DMH) masses from AGN–galaxy cross-correlation functions, and use a multivariate nearest-neighbour matching algorithm to isolate trends under controlled host-galaxy conditions.

## Key Findings (3–5 concise claims with evidence)

1. Typical halo mass for X-ray broad-line AGN is group-scale: log(M_DMH / h^{-1} M_☉) ≃ 13.
   - Evidence: AGN–galaxy cross-correlation measurements converted to halo masses (authors' clustering analysis; sample: 427 BLAGN vs >20k galaxies). The paper states the characteristic halo mass is ≃10^13 (h^{-1} M_☉) within the uncertainties of the dataset.

2. No statistically significant dependence of halo mass on black-hole mass, Eddington ratio, or X-ray luminosity.
   - Evidence: After applying a multivariate nearest-neighbour matching scheme to control for host-galaxy properties, the measured DMH masses are consistent across bins of M_BH, λ_Edd, and L_X (i.e., flat trends within uncertainties reported by the authors).

3. Large-scale environment is not the primary regulator of short-term accretion or long-term BH growth; internal host-galaxy processes dominate.
   - Evidence: The lack of an environmental trend (Claim 2) combined with matched host-galaxy controls suggests that internal factors (gas supply in the galaxy, secular processes) explain variations in accretion, while environment provides boundary conditions (gas reservoir, duty cycle).

4. Methodological note — the conclusions apply to the sample selection and redshift range studied.
   - Evidence / Limitations: The analysis focuses on X-ray-selected, broad-line AGN at 0.5 < z < 1.2 from XXL + Stripe82X; obscured AGN, other redshifts, or selection methods may show different environmental dependencies. Authors explicitly caution about sample selection when interpreting generality.

## Implications

- Supports scenarios where galaxy-scale physics (e.g., gas inflows, internal instabilities) set the instantaneous accretion behavior of AGN, while halo-scale structure modulates the available gas/duty-cycle but does not determine short-term AGN indicators like λ_Edd or L_X.
- Reinforces the need for matched host-galaxy controls when testing environmental effects on AGN.

## Methods & Data Notes

- Data: XXL + Stripe82X X-ray AGN samples; final AGN sample: 427 broad-line AGN in 0.5<z<1.2; galaxy catalog >20,000 objects.
- Environment estimator: AGN–galaxy cross-correlation functions → bias → halo mass (standard clustering-to-halo conversion).
- Control strategy: multivariate nearest-neighbour matching in host-galaxy parameter space to isolate BH/accretion effects.
