Title: Chemo-dynamical reconstruction of Milky Way globular cluster progenitors: age--metallicity relations and the universality of multiple stellar populations
Authors: Carmela Lardo; David Valcin; Raul Jimenez
arXiv: 2603.11814
DOI: https://doi.org/10.48550/arXiv.2603.11814
Submitted: 2026-03-12

Summary
-------
Using homogeneous stellar parameters for 69 Galactic globular clusters derived from hierarchical Bayesian color--magnitude-diagram (CMD) modelling of multiple populations, Lardo et al. (2026) reconstruct progenitor-specific age--metallicity relations (AMRs) and test whether helium-related multiple-population (MP) properties depend on progenitor origin once cluster mass and metallicity are controlled for.

Key points
- Ages, helium spreads (δY), mean helium abundances (Ȳ), and first-population fractions (f_P1) are inferred with hierarchical Bayesian CMD fitting.
- Enrichment timescales are constrained to τ ≲ 2 Gyr (typical), with individual progenitors sometimes preferring shorter values.
- Most progenitor AMRs reach Δ[Fe/H] ≃ 1.1–1.3 dex; Sagittarius reaches ≃1.6 dex and higher terminal metallicity.
- Neither δY nor Ȳ depend on progenitor origin; the mass–MP scaling (helium enrichment amplitude vs cluster mass) appears universal across in-situ and accreted systems.
- An exception: clusters associated with the Sequoia progenitor show higher f_P1 at fixed mass and metallicity, suggesting a secondary environmental imprint on the enriched-star fraction.

Key claims (3–5)
1. Helium enrichment amplitude (δY / Ȳ) scales primarily with cluster mass and is largely independent of progenitor origin — evidence for universal, cluster-scale MP formation physics.
2. Progenitor-specific AMRs retain fossil chemical-evolution signatures (extent and terminal metallicity) that reflect progenitor mass and enrichment history.
3. Enrichment (chemical-evolution) timescales are short, τ ≲ 2 Gyr, consistent with rapid early enrichment in progenitors.
4. Sequoia clusters show a residual difference in first-population fraction (f_P1), hinting environment can leave a secondary imprint on MP fractions.
