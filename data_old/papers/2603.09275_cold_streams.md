# 2603.09275 - Cold Gas Streams Fueling Galaxy Growth

**Status:** analyzed  
**Relevance:** high  
**Concepts:** cold-gas-streams, gas-accretion, cosmological-simulations, virial-shocks, star-formation-fueling

## Summary
Cosmological simulations show cold streams (T < 10^5 K) deliver gas directly to galaxy centers at z~2-4. Accretion rate Ṁ_stream ∝ M_halo^1.2. At z~2, streams contribute ~70% of gas accretion for massive galaxies, declining to ~30% by z~1.

## Key Quantities
- Stream temperature: T < 10^5 K
- Accretion rate: Ṁ_stream ∝ M_halo^1.2
- z~2 contribution: ~70% for massive galaxies
- z~1 contribution: ~30%
- Mechanism: bypass hot halo, direct delivery

## Galaxy Evolution Relevance
Cold streams sustain high SFRs at high-z. Their decline contributes to cosmic SFH decline. Efficient accretion channel.
