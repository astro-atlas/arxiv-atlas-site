# Merger-driven buildup of the M_BH - M_* relation bridging high-z overmassive black holes with the local relation

**Authors:** Takumi S. Tanaka, John D. Silverman, Kazuhiro Shimasaku, Knud Jahnke, Junyao Li, Makoto Ando

**arXiv ID:** 2603.11747

**Date:** 2026-03-12

**Status:** Preprint (9 pages, 8 figures)

## Abstract Summary

The authors test a non-causal merger-driven mechanism for producing the tight local M_BH–M_* relation via Monte Carlo simulations of merger-induced evolution of galaxies and their SMBHs. Starting from a highly scattered high-z population, they find mergers can reduce scatter to match the local relation; evolution of scatter is sensitive to merger mass ratios and rates. Minor mergers can contribute significantly to scatter evolution.

## Key Findings

1. Merger averaging can reproduce the local M_BH–M_* relation's low scatter starting from high-z scatter values (σ~0.8–1.0 dex).
2. The evolution of scatter depends strongly on merger mass ratio distribution and merger rate.
3. Minor mergers, if frequent, significantly affect scatter evolution.
4. Observational constraints at z~3–4 on σ and its mass dependence can test the scenario.
