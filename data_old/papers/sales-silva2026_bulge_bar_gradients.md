# Chemical radial gradients for the bulge-bar stellar populations from the APOGEE survey

**Authors:** J. V. Sales-Silva, K. Cunha, V. V. Smith, S. Daflon, D. Souto, R. Guerço, V. Loaiza-Tacuri, A. Queiroz, C. Chiappini, I. Minchev, S. R. Majewski, B. Barbuy, D. Bizyaev, J. G. Fernández-Trincado, P. M. Frinchaboy, S. Hasselquist, D. Horta, H. Jönsson, T. Masseron, N. Prantzos, R. P. Schiavon, M. Schultheis, M. Zoccali

**arXiv ID:** 2603.12070

**Date:** 2026-03-12

**Status:** Accepted for publication in ApJ

## Abstract Summary

Using APOGEE DR17 abundances and chemo-kinematical segregation of a bulge-bar sample, the authors identify six stellar populations in the inner Galaxy (two bar-driven, two high-eccentricity, two low-eccentricity), each split into low- and high-[Mg/Fe] sequences. They measure radial [X/H] gradients (for Mg, Si, Ca, Al, K, Mn, Co, Ni, Fe and the neutron-capture elements Ce and Nd) within R_Gal < 6 kpc and report distinct gradient behaviours for different populations [Sales-Silva et al. 2026, arXiv:2603.12070].

## Key Findings (3–5)

1. Population segregation: The bulge-bar sample separates into six chemo-kinematical populations (bar-driven, eccentric, low-eccentricity × low/high-[Mg/Fe]) based on orbital and abundance planes [Sales-Silva et al. 2026].

2. Different metallicity gradients by population: Low-[Mg/Fe] low-eccentricity stars show a slightly negative metallicity gradient, while high-[Mg/Fe] low-eccentricity stars show a slightly positive gradient — interpreted as a break in the global thin disk gradient [Sales-Silva et al. 2026].

3. Flat gradients for high-eccentricity populations: The high-eccentricity populations (both low- and high-[Mg/Fe]) exhibit approximately flat [Fe/H] gradients within the sampled region [Sales-Silva et al. 2026].

4. Element-by-element behaviour: For most elements [X/H] follows Fe, except the neutron-capture elements Ce and Nd which deviate from the Fe trend [Sales-Silva et al. 2026].

5. Bar-specific age/structure signal: The high-[Mg/Fe] bar population shows a much steeper positive [X/H] gradient compared to the nearly flat gradient of low-[Mg/Fe] bar stars; the authors suggest this reflects an age variation along the peanut structure and note consistency with N-body simulations and some high-redshift observations [Sales-Silva et al. 2026].

## Key Concepts

- Bulge-bar stellar populations [Sales-Silva et al. 2026]
- Radial metallicity gradients / metallicity gradients [Sales-Silva et al. 2026]
- [Mg/Fe] (α-element) sequences and disk–bulge connections [Sales-Silva et al. 2026]
- Neutron-capture element behaviour (Ce, Nd) [Sales-Silva et al. 2026]
- Peanut / boxy–peanut bulge structure and age gradients along the peanut [Sales-Silva et al. 2026]
- APOGEE survey and DR17 abundances [Sales-Silva et al. 2026]

## Implications

- Chemical gradients in the inner Galaxy are population-dependent; a single ‘‘bulge’’ gradient is insufficient to describe the inner region’s complexity [Sales-Silva et al. 2026].
- Positive gradients in high-[Mg/Fe] bar stars suggest internal age gradients along bar/peanut structures, with potential links to early rapid assembly or subsequent secular redistribution processes [Sales-Silva et al. 2026].
