# Balmer Decrements and Nebular-Stellar Reddening in JADES Galaxies at 2.7<z<7

**Authors:** Shreya Karthikeyan, Leonardo Clarke, Alice E. Shapley, Natalie Lam, Ryan L. Sanders, Naveen A. Reddy, Michael W. Topping, Gabriel B. Brammer

**arXiv ID:** 2603.11338

**Date:** 2026-03-11 (submitted)

**Status:** Submitted to ApJ

## Abstract Summary

The authors use JWST/NIRSpec measurements from the JADES survey to measure nebular and stellar reddening in star-forming galaxies at 2.7 ≤ z ≤ 7.0. The analysis uses 293 individual spectra and composite spectra incorporating 327 galaxies, deriving nebular reddening from Balmer decrements and stellar reddening from Prospector SED fits (Abstract; Section II). [Section II; Abstract]

## Key Findings (3–5 claims, each claim cites paper sections/figures)

1. Balmer decrement correlates with stellar mass in the mass-representative range at 2.7 < z ≤ 4.0. Evidence: binned medians and stacked spectra show Hα/Hβ rising with M⋆ with Pearson ρ = 0.462 (p = 1.2×10⁻¹⁰) in 2.7<z≤4.0 (Section III.1; Fig. 2). [Section III.1; Fig. 2]

2. At fixed stellar mass, the Balmer decrement shows little evolution out to z ∼ 7 in the mass-representative range. Evidence: comparison of medians and stacked measurements across redshift bins; medians/stacked Hα/Hβ are consistent within 1σ across bins (Section III.1; Fig. 3). [Section III.1; Fig. 3]

3. Differential reddening ΔE(B−V) ≡ E(B−V)_{gas} − E(B−V)_{star} scales roughly linearly with stellar mass and SFR at 2.7 ≤ z < 4.0 but shows no clear dependence on mass or SFR above z ≳ 4. Evidence: stacked spectra fits in Section III.2; reported slopes m_logM⋆ = 0.121 ± 0.011 mag dex⁻¹ and m_SFR100 = 0.122 ± 0.011 mag dex⁻¹ for 2.7≤z<4.0 (Section III.2; Figs. 4–5; Eqs. 3–4). [Section III.2; Figs. 4–5; Eqs. 3–4]

4. Nebular reddening correlates strongly with gas-phase metallicity out to z ∼ 5, while stellar reddening shows a weaker or absent correlation. Evidence: Pearson correlation tests and stacked points in Section III.3; ρ = 0.33 (p = 8.76×10⁻⁶) for nebular E(B−V) vs. 12+log(O/H) in 2.7<z≤4.0 (Section III.3; Fig. 6). [Section III.3; Fig. 6]

5. Practical prescriptions for converting E(B−V)_{star} to E(B−V)_{gas} are provided: (i) 2.7≤z<4.0: linear relations (Eqs. 3–4); (ii) 4.0≤z<5.0: constant offset +0.100; (iii) 5.0≤z<7.0: constant offset +0.053. These are calibrated on stacked spectra and are valid only over the sample's mass/SFR/redshift ranges (Section III.2; Eqs. 3–6). [Section III.2; Eqs. 3–6]

## Implications

- Dust geometry and dust mass both influence nebular vs. stellar reddening; the decreasing ΔE(B−V) at z ≳ 5 suggests nebular emission and stellar continuum probe more similar dust columns at earlier times (Discussion; Section IV). [Section IV]
- The non-evolution of the mass–reddening relation implies models must reproduce similar reddening at fixed mass out to z ∼ 7 (Section III.1; Section IV). [Section III.1; Section IV]

## Data & Methods Notes

- Sample selection and spectroscopy: JADES DR3 NIRCam + NIRSpec data, prism + gratings; emission-line fitting and SED fits with Prospector (Section II). [Section II]
- Nebular reddening: computed from Balmer decrement (Hα/Hβ or Hα/Hγ when necessary) assuming Cardelli et al. (1989) extinction and Case B ratios (Section II.3.1; Eq. 1). [Section II.3.1; Eq. 1]
- Metallicity: estimated from strong-line ratios using AURORA high-z calibrations (Section II.3.2). [Section II.3.2]
