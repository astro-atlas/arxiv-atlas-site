# Origin of open clusters revealed by the evolution of the m_max - M_ecl relation

**Authors:** J. W. Zhou, Sami Dib, Pavel Kroupa

**arXiv ID:** 2603.11782

**Date:** 2026-03-12

**Status:** Accepted for publication in MNRAS

## Abstract Summary

Using Gaia DR3 open cluster catalog, the authors identify the most massive star in each cluster and study the evolution of the m_max–M_cluster relation across ages. N-body simulations for individual cluster evolution and subcluster coalescence are compared. Coalescence models better match observations, showing systematically lower m_max–M_cluster relations, suggesting subcluster coalescence is a dominant pathway for open cluster formation.

## Key Findings

1. Observed m_max–M_cluster relation deviates with age; clusters >5 Myr show significant deviation from initial relation.
2. Coalescence simulations reproduce observed lower m_max–M_cluster relation better than isolated cluster models.
3. Gas expulsion mode affects evolutionary speed but not qualitative trajectory.
4. Results favor subcluster coalescence as a dominant formation pathway.
