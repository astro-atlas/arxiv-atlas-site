# 2603.09873 - ALMA Survey: Molecular Gas at z=0.5-3

**Status:** analyzed  
**Relevance:** high  
**Concepts:** molecular-gas, co-observations, gas-fraction, depletion-time, star-formation-efficiency, alma-survey

## Summary
ALMA CO observations of 156 galaxies at z=0.5-3 show molecular gas fraction increases as f_gas ∝ (1+z)^1.8, reaching ~50% at z~2. Depletion time remains constant at ~1 Gyr, indicating high-z star formation is driven by larger gas reservoirs, not enhanced efficiency.

## Key Quantities
- Sample: 156 galaxies in CO(3-2) and CO(4-3)
- Gas fraction evolution: f_gas ∝ (1+z)^1.8
- Gas fraction at z~2: ~50%
- Depletion time: ~1 Gyr (constant)

## Galaxy Evolution Relevance
Cosmic star formation history is driven by gas availability, not changing star formation physics. Gas accretion sustains high SFRs at early times.
