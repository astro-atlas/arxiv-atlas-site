# 2603.09670 - Metallicity Gradient Evolution

**Status:** analyzed  
**Relevance:** high  
**Concepts:** metallicity-gradients, inside-out-growth, chemical-evolution, spatially-resolved-spectroscopy, kmos-survey

## Summary
KMOS survey of 320 galaxies at z~0.5-2.5 shows metallicity gradients flatten with time: -0.4 at z~2 to -0.2 at z~0.5. Massive galaxies have steeper initial gradients. Evidence for inside-out growth.

## Key Quantities
- Sample: 320 galaxies at z~0.5-2.5
- Gradient evolution: -0.4 (z~2) → -0.2 (z~0.5)
- Mass dependence: massive galaxies have steeper gradients
- Mechanism: inside-out growth, late metal-poor gas accretion

## Galaxy Evolution Relevance
Metallicity gradients encode assembly history. Evolution supports inside-out growth with outer regions accreting metal-poor gas later.
