# GSE vs. LMC: reshaping of radially biased stellar haloes by satellites

**Authors:** Adam M. Dillamore, Jason L. Sanders, Richard A. N. Brooks

**arXiv ID:** 2603.11159

**Date:** 2026-03-11 (v1) — MNRAS submitted

## Abstract Summary

Dillamore et al. investigate how Large Magellanic Cloud (LMC) perturbations reshape the Milky Way's stellar halo, particularly focusing on the highly radially anisotropic Gaia Sausage-Enceladus (GSE) component. Using test-particle simulations of stellar haloes with different velocity anisotropies (β ∈ [0.5, 0.9]) perturbed by the LMC, they show that high-β orbits preferentially align with the LMC's tidal field, creating triaxial and tilted halo configurations. They propose that observed stellar overdensities (Virgo Overdensity, Hercules-Aquila Cloud) may arise from this LMC-driven dynamical alignment rather than preserving GSE merger geometry.

## Key findings (3–5 concise claims with evidence)

1. The LMC causes initially axisymmetric haloes to become approximately triaxial, with major axes tilted by ~14° out of the Galactic disc.
   - Evidence: Test particle simulations of haloes with β = 0.9 show transformation from axisymmetric to triaxial shapes; long axis aligns with LMC orbital plane; tilt angle increases monotonically with halo anisotropy (from ~6° at β=0.5 to ~13° at β=0.9).

2. Orbits with very high eccentricity (e ≳ 0.95) undergo azimuthal alignment with the LMC's tidal field on ~1 Gyr timescales; analytical theory predicts this alignment via pendulum-like equations of motion.
   - Evidence: Analytical model (Section 2) derives alignment criterion; minimum pendulum energy k²_min < 1 is satisfied for e ≳ 0.95 orbits. Numerical timescale τ_align ~ 0.3–1 Gyr for current LMC parameters; spectral clustering analysis reveals overdensities of ~40% in HAC/VOD regions after LMC passage.

3. Halo fractionation by anisotropy: the β = 0.9 component preferentially concentrates into two overdensities (HAC and VOD) while lower-β stars remain more uniformly distributed.
   - Evidence: Simulations starting with two populations (β = 0.5, 0.9) at equal density show final state dominated by β = 0.9 in HAC/VOD regions. Final HAC/VOD overdensities depend strongly on β; at β = 0.9, excess density reaches ~40% at 15 kpc.

4. HAC and VOD may not be relics of GSE merger geometry but rather products of LMC-driven orbital alignment; previous studies underestimated LMC perturbations by not considering sufficiently high anisotropy.
   - Evidence: Proposed mechanism (orbital alignment) naturally produces the observed spatial distribution and kinematic patterns (quadrupole pattern in Lz) without requiring tilted dark matter halo. Results call for revised interpretation of inner halo structure in equilibrium models.

## Methods & Data Notes

- Analytical model: pendulum formalism in Galactic isochrone potential; tidal force from LMC; derives alignment criterion and timescales.
- Simulations: test particle (N = 10^6) using Schwarzschild orbit-superposition models for haloes with fixed β; Milky Way potential from Vasiliev (2023); LMC integrated back 3.9 Gyr using Chandrasekhar dynamical friction.
- Halo models: doubly-broken power-law density (Han et al. 2022a parameters); axisymmetric initial conditions; β = 0.5, 0.6, ..., 0.9.
- Measured quantities: inertia tensor axis ratios (p, q); orientation angles; density overdensities in HAC/VOD regions.

## Implications

- LMC perturbations must be accounted for in equilibrium models of inner halo (10–25 kpc).
- Previous tilted dark matter halo interpretations (Han+2022b) may be unnecessary; LMC + high-β stars can explain observed tilt.
- HAC and VOD may have dynamical rather than merger-origin explanations; informs interpretations of stellar streams and halo structure.
