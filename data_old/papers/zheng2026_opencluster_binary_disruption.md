# Binary disruption during the early phase of open clusters

**Authors:** Zepeng Zheng, Long Wang, Holger Baumgardt

**arXiv ID:** 2603.11803

**Date:** 2026-03-12

**Status:** Accepted for publication in ApJ

## Abstract Summary

The binary fraction in young open clusters exceeds that of field stars. Using N-body simulations tailored to Gaia DR3 open cluster observations and assuming a 100% primordial binary fraction, the authors study early binary survival. They find a two-stage disruption: a rapid early decline followed by slower decrease. The early disruption rate scales with initial cluster density (~ρ0^0.56) and transition time scales as ~ρ0^-0.46. High-q and wide binaries are disrupted faster; eccentricity dependence is weak. They provide a Python tool to predict survival fractions and discuss contributions of cluster-escaped stars to the field binary population.

## Key Findings

1. Two-stage binary disruption with early fast decline and later slower phase.
2. Early disruption rate k1 ∝ ρ0^0.56; transition time tb ∝ ρ0^-0.46.
3. High mass-ratio and wide binaries disrupted more rapidly.
4. Escaped stars have lower binary fraction than cluster-retained stars; lower-q systems preferentially remain bound.
