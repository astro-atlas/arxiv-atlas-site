# 2603.09540 - Galaxy Stellar Mass Function in COSMOS

**Status:** analyzed  
**Relevance:** high  
**Concepts:** stellar-mass-function, cosmos-survey, mass-assembly, hierarchical-formation, schechter-function

## Summary
COSMOS measurements of GSMF from z~0.2 to z~3 show characteristic mass increases as log M* ∝ 0.2(1+z), faint-end slope α ≈ -1.3 (constant). Stellar mass density increases by factor of ~10 from z~3 to z~0.

## Key Quantities
- Redshift range: z~0.2 to z~3
- Characteristic mass evolution: log M* ∝ 0.2(1+z)
- Faint-end slope: α ≈ -1.3 (constant)
- Mass density growth: factor of ~10 (z~3 to z~0)
- Declining growth at z<1

## Galaxy Evolution Relevance
GSMF evolution provides fundamental constraints on hierarchical galaxy formation. Mass assembly slows at late times.
