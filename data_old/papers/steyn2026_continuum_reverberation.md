Title: Continuum Reverberation in Bright Quasars Using NASA/ATLAS
Authors: Zachary Steyn; Christian Wolf; Christopher Onken; et al.
arXiv: 2603.11789
DOI: https://doi.org/10.48550/arXiv.2603.11789
Submitted: 2026-03-12

Summary
-------
Steyn et al. (2026) analyse high-cadence light curves of 9,498 bright quasars (z=0.3–2.5) to measure inter-band continuum lags (continuum reverberation) and probe accretion-disk structure. They find the well-known disk-size discrepancy (lags ≈ 3× larger than thin-disk predictions) persists at high luminosities; the previously reported anti-correlation with luminosity is likely driven by wavelength effects. The wavelength dependence of delays is complex and non-monotonic, implicating contamination from variable diffuse emission (e.g., BLR/dust). Longer lags correlate with higher Eddington ratio, redder colours, stronger FeII, and other quasar properties.

Key claims
1. The disk-size discrepancy (observed continuum lags ≳ thin-disk predictions) persists across a large, luminous quasar sample.
2. The apparent anti-correlation of lag amplitude with luminosity is likely a wavelength-driven effect, not intrinsic disk size scaling.
3. Variable diffuse emission contaminates inter-band delays widely across quasars, producing complex wavelength dependence.
4. Longer lags are found in systems with higher Eddington ratio, redder colours, stronger FeII, and other specific spectroscopic signatures.
