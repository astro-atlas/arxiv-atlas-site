# Blue-dominant Lyman-alpha (Lyα) emission as evidence of gas inflow in ultra-low-mass galaxies

**Authors:** Tamal Mukherjee, Zhihui Li, Tayyaba Zafar, Themiya Nanayakkara, Davide Tornotti, Luca Costantin, Aalia Imam Uzma

**arXiv ID:** 2603.11411

**Date:** 2026-03-12

**Status:** Submitted to A&A

## Abstract Summary

We report the detection of a clumpy, blue-dominated Lyman-α (Lyα) emission at z = 3.066 embedded in a cosmic-web filament in the MUSE eXtremely Deep Field (MXDF), spatially associated with a primary galaxy and two compact ultra-low-mass star-forming components. Radiative-transfer modelling of the spatially-resolved Lyα profiles using a multiphase clumpy model favours radial inflow of neutral clumps with v_cl ≈ 98 km s⁻¹. SED fitting to deep JWST/NIRCam photometry indicates the two compact components have ultra-low stellar masses (log(M*/M☉) ≈ 6.3–6.9) and very young mass-weighted ages (&lt;~7 Myr), high specific SFRs and elevated ionisation parameters — consistent with recent bursty star formation likely fuelled by accreting gas. (Summary based on Mukherjee et al. 2026, arXiv:2603.11411)

## Key Findings

1. Radiative-transfer modelling of spatially-resolved Lyα profiles (multiphase clumpy model) finds a radial inflow velocity of clumps v_cl ≈ 98 km s⁻¹ (Mukherjee et al. 2026; §3.2; Table 1; Fig. 2). Evidence: best-fit model to Voronoi-binned Lyα spectra (Sect. 3.1–3.2) reproduces a blue-dominant double-peaked profile with B/T ≈ 0.65.

2. The Lyα emission is extended and clumpy, spatially offset from the primary galaxy’s continuum peak and embedded in a 4 cMpc cosmic-web filament (Sect. 2.1; Fig. 1; Sect. 4.4). Evidence: MUSE narrow-band Lyα image and measured offset (0.66 arcsec) between primary continuum and Lyα SB peak.

3. Two compact components (primary–1 and the companion) are ultra-low-mass (log(M*/M☉) ≈ 6.34–6.91), extremely young (mass-weighted ages ≲7 Myr) and bursty (sSFR ≳ 10⁻⁸ yr⁻¹; Sect. 3.3, 4.2; Table 2; Fig. 6). Evidence: resolved JWST/NIRCam photometry + BAGPIPES SED fits and Hα-based SFRs.

4. NFW halo modelling indicates the companion resides well inside the virial region of the primary halo and survives as a coherent sub-halo (Sect. 4.3; Fig. 4). Evidence: stellar-to-halo mass conversion + concentration–mass relation (Colossus) produce combined NFW density profile dominated by the primary.

5. Interpretation: The spatial coincidence of blue-dominant Lyα inflow signatures with extremely young, low-mass, high-sSFR components supports a scenario in which cold gas accretion (from the cosmic web or recycled gas) is fueling localized starbursts (Sect. 4.4–4.5).
