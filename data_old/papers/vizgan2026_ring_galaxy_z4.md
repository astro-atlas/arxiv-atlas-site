# A clumpy ring galaxy at z=4.0148

**Authors:** David Vizgan, Ming-Yang Zhuang, Ian Smail, Rogier A. Windhorst, et al.

**arXiv ID:** 2603.11575

**Date:** 2026-03-15

**Survey:** JWST PEARLS

## Abstract Summary

This paper presents HST/ACS, JWST/NIRCam, and JWST/NIRSpec observations of a candidate ring galaxy at z_spec=4.0148, previously identified as a potential gravitational lens. If confirmed as a ring galaxy, this would be the most distant ring galaxy discovered to date.

### Key Properties:

1. **Morphology:**
   - Complex structure with three bright clumps along an apparent ring
   - Ring radius ≈0.25" ≈ 1.8 kpc (smaller than other high-z rings)
   - Red center surrounded by blue ring
   - Five faint companions within ~1" radius
   - Apparent tidal tail extending from ring edge

2. **Physical Properties:**
   - Total SFR = 140⁺²⁰₋₃₀ M⊙ yr⁻¹
   - log(M*/M⊙) = 10.41⁺⁰·¹¹₋₀.₁₃
   - Dust mass ≈ (9±4)×10⁷ M⊙ from ALMA detection
   - Three main clumps have ~1/3 mass but >2× SFR of center

3. **Spectroscopic Confirmation:**
   - NIRSpec PRISM spectrum shows Hα+[NII], [SII], HeI, [SIII], faint Lyα
   - Redshift z=4.0148 from CANUCS DR1
   - Photometric redshifts of clumps agree (z~3.89-4.19)

### Ring Galaxy vs. Gravitational Lens:

**Evidence for Ring Galaxy:**
- Photometric redshifts of all components consistent with z~4
- Different colors of clumps hard to explain by lensing
- Two companions at similar redshift along tidal tail

**Evidence Against Lensing:**
- If foreground lens at z~1.66, would require unrealistic dark matter fraction (~95%)
- Stellar-to-total mass ratio (~4-6%) much lower than known JWST lenses
- No strong emission lines in NIRSpec data support z~1.7 solution

### Comparison to Other High-z Ring Galaxies:

**Similarities:**
- Clumpy structure (common in high-z rings)
- High SFR, lies on/above star-forming main sequence
- Similar stellar mass to other high-z rings

**Differences:**
- Smaller radius (1.8 kpc vs typical 3-10 kpc)
- No obvious "bullet" galaxy companion
- Possible candidates: distant galaxy at 125 kpc, low-mass companions, or clump A

### Implications:

1. **For Galaxy Evolution:**
   - Ring galaxies may be more common at high-z than expected
   - Could trace merger rates across cosmic time
   - Selection effects favor star-forming rings at high-z

2. **For Lens Surveys:**
   - Ring galaxies are significant contaminants
   - Cannot distinguish from lenses based on Einstein radius alone
   - Future surveys (Euclid, LSST) need to account for this contamination
