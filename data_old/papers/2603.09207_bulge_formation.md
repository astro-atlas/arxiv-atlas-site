# 2603.09207 - Bulge Formation in Disk Galaxies

**Status:** analyzed  
**Relevance:** high  
**Concepts:** bulge-formation, pseudobulges, classical-bulges, secular-evolution, mergers, bulge-to-total-ratio

## Summary
Study of 800 local disk galaxies shows pseudobulges (secular) comprise 60% of bulges in late-type spirals, classical bulges (mergers) dominate in early-types. Transition at Hubble type Sbc.

## Key Quantities
- Sample: 800 local disk galaxies
- Pseudobulge fraction: 60% in late-type spirals
- Classical bulge dominance: early-types
- Transition: Hubble type Sbc
- Pseudobulges: lower B/T, disk-like properties

## Galaxy Evolution Relevance
Both secular processes and mergers contribute to bulge formation. Relative importance depends on galaxy type. Multiple formation pathways exist.
