# 2603.09828 - Bars Driving Central Star Formation

**Status:** analyzed  
**Relevance:** high  
**Concepts:** stellar-bars, secular-evolution, central-star-formation, gas-inflow, disk-galaxies

## Summary
SDSS study of 2,500 disk galaxies shows barred galaxies have 1.5-2× higher central SFR than unbarred. Effect strongest in intermediate-mass galaxies (M* ~ 10^10-10^11 M☉). Central SFR enhancement correlates with bar strength.

## Key Quantities
- Sample: 2,500 disk galaxies
- Central SFR enhancement: 1.5-2× in barred galaxies
- Strongest effect: M* ~ 10^10-10^11 M☉
- Correlation: central SFR ∝ bar strength (ellipticity)

## Galaxy Evolution Relevance
Bars drive secular evolution by funneling gas to centers, fueling star formation and potentially building bulges.
