# 2603.09805 - Environment Impact on Tully-Fisher Relation

**Status:** analyzed  
**Relevance:** high  
**Concepts:** tully-fisher-relation, environmental-effects, tidal-stripping, dark-matter-halos, scaling-relations

## Summary
Study of 800 spirals across environments shows cluster galaxies have lower rotation velocities at fixed mass (Δlog V_rot ≈ -0.08 dex) than field galaxies. Consistent with tidal stripping of dark matter halos in dense environments.

## Key Quantities
- Sample: 800 spiral galaxies
- Cluster offset: Δlog V_rot ≈ -0.08 dex at fixed mass
- Mechanism: tidal stripping of dark matter halos
- Evolution: environment-dependent TFR zero-point evolution

## Galaxy Evolution Relevance
Environmental processes modify galaxy halos, affecting fundamental scaling relations. Tidal stripping in clusters is observable.
