# The Kinematically Hot, Extremely Metal-Poor C-19 Stellar Stream in DESI DR2

**Authors:** Nasser Mohammed, Joseph Y. Tang, Ting S. Li, Sergey E. Koposov, Raymond G. Carlberg, Emma Jarvis, Andrew P. Li, Nathan Sandford, Gustavo E. Medina, Wenting Wang, Monica Valluri, Alexander H. Riley, Leandro Beraldo e Silva, Joan Najita, Mika Lambert, Songting Li, and DESI Collaboration

**arXiv ID:** 2603.11171

**Date:** 2026-03-11

**Status:** Submitted to ApJ

## Abstract Summary

Mohammed et al. characterize the C-19 stellar stream using DESI DR2 spectroscopy, identifying 47 confirmed member stars (41 newly discovered) with radial velocities, proper motions, and metallicities. The C-19 stream is an extremely metal-poor ([Fe/H] = -3.36) stellar population with a large velocity dispersion, difficult to reconcile with a simple globular cluster (GC) progenitor model.

## Key Findings (3–5 claims with evidence)

1. **C-19 stream is kinematically hot with velocity dispersion σ_v = 7.8 ± 1.5 km/s:**
   - Much larger than expected for a conventional GC disruption scenario
   - Measured from 47 spectroscopically confirmed stream members via radial velocities and proper motions
   - Evidence: DESI DR2 spectroscopy combined with proper motions; mixture model characterization in 3D (PM, RV, [Fe/H])

2. **Extremely metal-poor stream: [Fe/H] = -3.36 ± 0.12 (mean):**
   - Chemical abundance pattern characteristic of a globular cluster
   - Despite kinematic properties inconsistent with simple GC model
   - Evidence: Metallicity measurements from DESI spectra; comparison to GC populations

3. **47 member stars identified; 41 are newly discovered:**
   - Previous literature: only 6 known members
   - DESI's faint magnitude limit and radial velocity measurements essential for discovery
   - Evidence: Mixture model approach jointly characterizing proper motions, RVs, and [Fe/H] against MW halo background

4. **Novel 'spur' feature identified within the stream:**
   - Sub-structure observed in spatial, kinematic, or metallicity distribution
   - May indicate additional complexity in progenitor or disruption scenario
   - Evidence: Spatial/kinematic analysis of stream member distribution

## Implications

- Standard models of GC disruption may be incomplete; other scenarios (merger remnant, multiple progenitors, or modified dynamical evolution) may be needed to explain kinematic properties
- DESI spectroscopy enables population-level studies of stellar streams; future work will apply this to many streams in DESI footprint
- Stellar streams probe galactic potential and disruption physics; ultra-metal-poor streams may originate from early Galactic assembly
