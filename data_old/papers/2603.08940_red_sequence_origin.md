# 2603.08940 - Origin of Red Sequence: Environment vs. Mass Quenching

**Status:** analyzed  
**Relevance:** high  
**Concepts:** red-sequence, environmental-quenching, mass-quenching, gama-survey, quenched-galaxies

## Summary
GAMA survey of 50,000 galaxies shows mass quenching dominates for M* > 10^10.5 M☉ at z~0.1. Environmental quenching important at lower masses and in dense environments. Cluster quenched fraction 40% higher than field at fixed mass.

## Key Quantities
- Sample: 50,000 galaxies
- Mass quenching dominance: M* > 10^10.5 M☉
- Cluster vs. field: 40% higher quenched fraction
- Environment as additional channel: beyond mass effects
- Redshift: z~0.1

## Galaxy Evolution Relevance
Both mass and environment contribute to quenching. Environment provides additional mechanism beyond mass-driven processes.
