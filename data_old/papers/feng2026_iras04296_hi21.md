# H I 21-cm Line Properties of the Nearby LIRG IRAS 04296+2923

**Authors:** Guixiang Feng, Zhongzu Wu, Chuanpeng Zhang, Ming Zhu

**arXiv ID:** 2603.11899

**Date:** 2026-03-12

**Status:** Accepted by A&A

## Abstract Summary

The authors analyze archival VLA and FAST H I 21-cm imaging together with multi-band radio continuum data for the nearby luminous infrared galaxy (LIRG) IRAS 04296+2923 (distance ≈29 Mpc). The system is embedded in a small group with five H I-detected members; the closest companion HI 0432+2926 lies at projected ≈40 kpc with a small line-of-sight velocity offset (Δv ≈ 26 km s−1). Both main galaxies show regular H I velocity fields and double-horn profiles consistent with rotation-dominated kinematics, with only subtle asymmetries and extended H I structure. Radio continuum is detected only from IRAS 04296+2923, concentrated in its nucleus, and spectral modeling indicates a significant free–free component at &gt;30 GHz and a high FIR-to-radio flux ratio implying a compact, young, dust-obscured nuclear starburst (Feng et al. 2026, arXiv:2603.11899).

## Key Findings

1. Group environment: H I imaging reveals five group members around IRAS 04296+2923, supporting a small-group environment rather than isolation (Feng et al. 2026).

2. Companion and kinematics: The close companion HI 0432+2926 has comparable H I mass (~10^9 M_sun) and a small velocity offset (Δv ≈ 26 km s−1); both show rotation-dominated H I velocity fields with double-horn profiles (Feng et al. 2026).

3. Nuclear activity and radio continuum: Radio continuum emission is confined to the nucleus of IRAS 04296+2923, with a high q_8.4 (≈3.2) and evidence for significant free–free emission at high radio frequencies, pointing to a young, compact starburst rather than a dominant AGN (Feng et al. 2026).

4. Interaction vs internal processes: The authors argue that the LIRG-level starburst is plausibly driven by internal processes (e.g., bar-induced inflow) possibly modulated by long-timescale, low-level tidal interactions with group companions, rather than an advanced major merger (Feng et al. 2026).
