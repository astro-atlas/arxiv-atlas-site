# A systematic study of AGN feedback in a disk galaxy I: global overview

**Authors:** Yuxuan Zou, Feng Yuan, Suoqing Ji, Luis C. Ho, Yingjie Peng, Jing Wang, Bocheng Zhu, Tao Wang

**arXiv ID:** 2603.11179

**Date:** 2026-03-11

**DOI:** 10.3847/1538-4357/ae47fe

**Status:** Published in ApJ 1000, 41 (2026)

## Abstract Summary

Zou et al. use the MACER cosmological simulation framework to investigate AGN feedback in a disk galaxy, including cosmological inflows. They simulate the co-evolution of AGN and star formation over Gyr timescales, focusing on the role of cold filaments condensing in the circumgalactic medium.

## Key Findings (3–5 claims with evidence)

1. **Predicted AGN duty cycle of ~0.49% matches observations.**
   - MACER simulations show consistent AGN duty cycle with observed AGN fractions
   - Evidence: comparison of simulated duty cycle to observational estimates

2. **Positive correlation between AGN luminosity and star formation rate (SFR):**
   - AGN luminosity and SFR light curves show positive correlation despite feedback
   - Driven by cold filaments condensing in CGM and falling onto the galaxy
   - Evidence: time series analysis of simulated AGN luminosity and SFR

3. **Cold filaments in CGM enhance both SFR and AGN activity:**
   - Radiative cooling drives formation of massive cold filaments in circumgalactic medium
   - Infall of this material fuels intense starburst episodes and AGN activity
   - Models with AGN feedback produce higher peak SFRs than models without it
   - Evidence: gas temperature and density profiles in CGM; mass accretion rates

4. **AGN feedback quenches the galaxy over ~1 Gyr timescale:**
   - Strong feedback from enhanced AGN activity (following intense starbursts) removes gas and quenches star formation
   - This demonstrates that SFR-AGN correlation does NOT preclude AGN feedback as quenching agent
   - Evidence: evolution of SFR and AGN luminosity; gas removal and heating

## Implications

- Cold-mode accretion and CGM dynamics are central to AGN-SFR connection
- AGN feedback can simultaneously drive starbursts and quench galaxies (two phases)
- Models must account for cosmological inflows and multi-scale feedback to explain galaxy evolution
