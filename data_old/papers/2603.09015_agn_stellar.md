# 2603.09015 - AGN Feedback Impact on Stellar Populations

**Status:** analyzed  
**Relevance:** high  
**Concepts:** agn-feedback, stellar-populations, quenching, alpha-enhancement, massive-galaxies

## Summary
Study of 200 massive galaxies at z~0.5-1 shows AGN hosts have older stellar populations by ~1-2 Gyr and higher [α/Fe] ratios. Enhanced α-enhancement indicates rapid star formation prior to quenching.

## Key Quantities
- Sample: 200 massive galaxies (M* > 10^11 M☉)
- Age difference: ~1-2 Gyr (AGN vs. control)
- [α/Fe] enhancement: higher in AGN hosts
- Redshift: z~0.5-1
- Mechanism: rapid quenching by AGN feedback

## Galaxy Evolution Relevance
AGN feedback leaves observable signatures in stellar populations. Constrains timing and nature of quenching in massive galaxies.
