# 2603.09340 - Star Formation Main Sequence Turnover at z~4-6

**Status:** analyzed  
**Relevance:** high  
**Concepts:** star-formation-main-sequence, turnover, early-quenching, high-redshift, jwst-observations

## Summary
HST+JWST study of HUDF/GOODS shows SFMS turnover at M* ≈ 10^10.5 M☉ at z~4-6. SFR-mass relation flattens/declines at higher masses. SFMS normalization evolves as (1+z)^2.5.

## Key Quantities
- Redshift range: z~4-6
- Turnover mass: M* ≈ 10^10.5 M☉
- Normalization evolution: ∝ (1+z)^2.5
- Turnover: SFR-mass flattens/declines at high masses
- Evidence for: early quenching in massive galaxies

## Galaxy Evolution Relevance
SFMS turnover at high-z suggests massive galaxies begin quenching earlier than thought. Constraints on quenching timing.
