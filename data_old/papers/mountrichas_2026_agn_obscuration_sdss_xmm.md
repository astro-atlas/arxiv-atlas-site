# AGN obscuration in optical and X-rays: Host properties and the interplay of nuclear and galactic gas and dust in a combined SDSS-XMM sample

**Authors:** G. Mountrichas, F. J. Carrera, E. Quintin, A. Viitanen, A. Corral, N. Webb

**arXiv ID:** 2603.11180

**Date:** 2026-03-11

**Status:** Accepted to A&A

## Abstract Summary

Mountrichas et al. investigate the connection between optical AGN classification (broad-line vs. narrow-line) and X-ray absorption properties (NH column density) in 241 X-ray selected AGN at z<1.9. They combine XMM-Newton X-ray spectroscopy with SDSS optical spectroscopy and SED fitting to measure host-galaxy properties.

## Key Findings (3–5 claims with evidence)

1. **Most AGN show optical-X-ray agreement (~70%), but two mismatched populations exist:**
   - Broad-Line AGN with X-ray absorption (BLAbs): 11 sources showing elevated gas-to-dust ratios; inconsistent with standard orientation model
   - Narrow-Line Unobscured AGN (NLUnabs): 58 sources appearing unobscured in X-rays but classified as NL optically; ~50% assigned type-1 properties by SED fitting
   - Evidence: sample of 241 X-ray AGN; 172 (~70%) show agreement per classification

2. **BLAbs AGN have elevated gas-to-dust ratios and lower dust content:**
   - Suggests dust-free or host-scale absorbers rather than nuclear torus absorption
   - Implies obscuration is multi-scale, not purely orientation-driven
   - Evidence: gas-to-dust ratio measurements from SED fitting (via CIGALE)

3. **Optical Balmer decrements and ionization conditions vary between NL and NLUnabs populations:**
   - NL AGN show higher Balmer decrements than NLUnabs, indicating stronger extinction and different ionization conditions
   - Suggests intrinsically weak broad-line regions, host contamination, or AGN variability in NLUnabs
   - Evidence: optical line diagnostics from SDSS spectra

4. **Host galaxy properties differ: NLUnabs resemble BL AGN; NL AGN are quiescent:**
   - At z<0.8, NLUnabs: star-formation rates and accretion efficiencies comparable to BL AGN
   - NL AGN: reside in more quiescent hosts with lower star formation and less efficient black-hole growth
   - BLAbs: match BL AGN in host properties but with excess X-ray absorption
   - Evidence: SED-derived SFR and accretion efficiency measurements

## Implications

- Obscuration in AGN arises from multi-scale gas/dust distributions, not orientation alone
- Mismatched optical-X-ray populations complicate AGN demographic interpretation in large surveys (Euclid, LSST)
- Need for careful host contamination and variability assessment in optical classification
