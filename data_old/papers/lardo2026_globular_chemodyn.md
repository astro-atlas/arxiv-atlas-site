# Chemo-dynamical reconstruction of Milky Way globular cluster progenitors: age--metallicity relations and the universality of multiple stellar populations

**Authors:** Carmela Lardo, David Valcin, Raul Jimenez

**arXiv ID:** 2603.11814

**Date:** 2026-03-12

**Status:** Submitted to A&A

## Abstract Summary

Globular clusters encode the hierarchical assembly history of the Milky Way and the physics of multiple stellar populations. Using homogeneous stellar parameters for 69 Galactic globular clusters derived while modelling multiple populations, the authors reconstruct progenitor-specific age--metallicity relations (AMRs) and test whether helium-related multiple-population (MP) properties depend on progenitor origin once cluster mass and metallicity are controlled for.

### Key Findings:

1. Enrichment timescales are short: τ ≲ 2 Gyr, with some progenitors preferring shorter values when fitted independently.
2. Most progenitors reach Δ[Fe/H] ≈ 1.1–1.3 dex; Sagittarius reaches ~1.6 dex and higher terminal metallicities.
3. Gaia–Sausage–Enceladus and low-energy/Kraken are identified as dominant accretion events shaping the GC system.
4. Helium-related MP indicators (δY, mean Y) do not depend on progenitor origin once mass and metallicity are controlled; mass–MP scaling is universal across in-situ and accreted systems.
5. Sequoia clusters show a higher first-population fraction (f_P1) at fixed mass and metallicity — a possible residual environmental imprint.

## Implications

- AMRs preserve fossil signatures of progenitor chemical evolution and mass hierarchy.
- The universality of helium enrichment amplitude with mass suggests cluster-scale formation physics dominate over large-scale environment.
- The exception in f_P1 hints that the fraction of enriched stars may retain a secondary dependence on progenitor environment.
