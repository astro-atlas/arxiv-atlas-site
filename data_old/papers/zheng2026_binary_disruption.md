Title: Binary disruption during the early phase of open clusters
Authors: Zepeng Zheng; Long Wang; Holger Baumgardt
arXiv: 2603.11803
DOI: https://doi.org/10.48550/arXiv.2603.11803
Submitted: 2026-03-12

Summary
-------
Using N-body simulations informed by Gaia DR3 open-cluster observations and assuming a 100% primordial binary fraction, Zheng et al. (2026) study the early evolution of binary survival fractions in open clusters. They find a two-stage disruption: (1) an initial rapid decline and (2) a slower decrease, which are well described by piecewise linear functions. The early disruption rate (k1) scales with the cluster's initial density (ρ0) as a power law with index ≃0.56; the transition time tb scales as ρ0^(-0.46). Binary disruption depends strongly on binary parameters: high-q and wide binaries are disrupted faster; eccentricity dependence is weaker. The authors released a Python tool to predict binary survival evolution and discuss implications for the field binary population (escaped stars have lower binary fraction likely due to mass segregation).

Key claims
1. Binary disruption in young open clusters occurs in two stages: a rapid early decline followed by a slower decline.
2. The early disruption rate k1 follows a power-law with initial cluster density (index ≃0.56), and the transition time tb scales as ρ0^(-0.46).
3. Binary parameter dependence: high mass-ratio (q) and wide binaries are preferentially disrupted early.
4. Escaped/field stars show a systematically lower binary fraction, influenced by mass segregation.
