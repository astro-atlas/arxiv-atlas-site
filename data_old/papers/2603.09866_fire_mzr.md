# 2603.09866 - Origin of Mass-Metallicity Relation in FIRE

**Status:** analyzed  
**Relevance:** high  
**Concepts:** mass-metallicity-relation, fire-simulations, chemical-evolution, gas-accretion, metal-retention, feedback

## Summary
FIRE simulations show the MZR arises from potential well depth (retaining metals) and gas accretion history (diluting metals). Low-mass galaxies have shallow potentials and high accretion rates → metal dilution. Massive galaxies retain metals effectively.

## Key Quantities
- Simulation: FIRE cosmological zoom-ins
- MZR driver: potential depth + gas accretion
- Low-mass: shallow potentials, high accretion → dilution
- Massive: deep potentials, self-regulated SF → retention

## Galaxy Evolution Relevance
MZR emerges from fundamental gravitational and gas dynamics, not solely feedback. Physical explanation for observed metallicity trends.
