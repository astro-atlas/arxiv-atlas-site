# 2603.08996 - Galaxy Size Evolution z~3 to z~0

**Status:** analyzed  
**Relevance:** high  
**Concepts:** size-evolution, mass-size-relation, minor-mergers, candels-survey, 3d-hst

## Summary
CANDELS+3D-HST study of 10,000 galaxies shows mass-dependent size evolution. Low-mass galaxies (M* ~ 10^9.5 M☉) grow by factor of ~3, massive galaxies (M* ~ 10^11.5 M☉) by only ~30%. Most growth at z>1.

## Key Quantities
- Sample: 10,000 galaxies
- Low-mass growth: factor of ~3
- Massive growth: ~30%
- Peak growth epoch: z>1
- Mechanism: minor merger-driven

## Galaxy Evolution Relevance
Size evolution is mass-dependent. Low-mass galaxies experience more mergers relative to mass. Different growth mechanisms at different masses.
