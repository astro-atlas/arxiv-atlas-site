# Comparison of Bar Formation Mechanisms. IIIA. The role of classical bulges in spontaneous bar formation

**Authors:** Yirui Zheng, Juntai Shen, Bin-Hui Chen

**arXiv ID:** 2603.12121 

**Date:** 2026-03-12

**Status:** Submitted to ApJ

## Abstract Summary

This N-body simulation study investigates how classical bulges affect bar formation through the internal (spontaneous) mechanism. The authors incorporate bulges of varying mass and compactness into disk galaxy models and evolve them in isolation.

### Key Findings:

1. **Bulge Effects on Bar Formation:**
   - More massive/compact bulges increase Toomre Q stability parameter
   - Increase circular velocity in central regions
   - Decrease disk mass fraction
   - Result: Delayed bar formation onset and increased growth timescale
   - Sufficiently strong bulges can suppress bar formation entirely

2. **Pattern Speed Evolution:**
   - During formation: Bars with more massive/compact bulges show:
     - Higher initial pattern speeds
     - Faster deceleration rates
   - This faster deceleration persists after bar buckling
   - Result: Slower-rotating bars in secular growth stage

3. **Secular Growth Stage:**
   - When bulge's "diluting" effect on bar strength is removed
   - All bars within same disk show similar:
     - Pattern speed-bar strength (Ωₚ-A₂) distributions
     - Co-rotation to bar length ratios (R = R_CR/R_bar)
   - Suggests disk dominates pattern speed evolution in this stage

### Implications:
- Bulge influence is most significant during bar formation stage
- In secular growth stage, disk component dominates pattern speed evolution
- Bulge mass and compactness are key parameters for bar suppression
