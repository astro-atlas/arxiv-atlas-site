# Energy dependence of the X-ray power spectrum in NGC4051 and NGC4395

**Authors:** V. A. Diamantopoulos, I. E. Papadakis, A. Akylas, A. Zoghbi, E. Kammoun, B. Rani

**arXiv ID:** 2603.11825

**Date:** 2026-03-12

**Status:** Published in A&A (A&A, 707, A180, 2026); DOI: 10.1051/0004-6361/202557606

## Abstract Summary

The authors analyse archival XMM-Newton, Suzaku, and NuSTAR light curves of two highly variable, low-mass Seyferts (NGC 4051 and NGC 4395) to study the energy dependence of the X-ray power spectral density (PSD). Using six energy bands across 0.3–20 keV and fitting a bending power-law model, they find that (i) the PSD bending frequency is consistent with being energy-independent, (ii) the high-frequency slope becomes flatter at higher photon energies, and (iii) the PSD amplitude decreases with increasing energy. These results constrain physical models of X-ray variability (e.g., fluctuating accretion-rate models) and motivate larger-sample studies.

## Key Findings

1. PSD bending frequency shows no significant dependence on photon energy for both NGC4051 and NGC4395 (Diamantopoulos et al. 2026, A&A).

2. The high-frequency PSD slope flattens at harder X-ray energies, indicating changes in short-timescale variability with energy (Diamantopoulos et al. 2026, A&A).

3. The PSD amplitude systematically decreases with increasing photon energy, implying less fractional variability at harder bands (Diamantopoulos et al. 2026, A&A).
