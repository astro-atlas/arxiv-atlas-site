# Continuum Reverberation in Bright Quasars Using NASA/ATLAS

**Authors:** Zachary Steyn, Christian Wolf, Christopher Onken, Ken Smith, Ji-Jia Tang, Andjelka B. Kovacevic, John Tonry, Alejandro Clocchiatti

**arXiv ID:** 2603.11789

**Date:** 2026-03-12

**Status:** Submitted to MNRAS

## Abstract Summary

High-cadence light curves for 9,498 bright quasars (0.3 < z < 2.5) were analyzed to measure inter-band continuum delays and probe accretion disk sizes via reverberation mapping. The study finds that measured delays remain ~3× larger than standard thin-disk theory predicts even at high luminosity. Wavelength dependence of delays is complex and suggests widespread contamination from variable diffuse emission. Longer lags are associated with higher Eddington ratios, redder colours, stronger FeII, presence of CIV BALs, and lower CIV blueshift.

## Key Findings

1. Continuum inter-band delays ~3× larger than standard thin-disk predictions persist in high-luminosity quasars.
2. Wavelength dependence is complex; diffuse emission contamination is common.
3. Longer lags correlate with higher Eddington ratio, redder colours, stronger FeII, CIV BALs, and lower CIV blueshift.
